Punjab III: Sialkot, Lahore (Oct - Nov 1897)

1 31 Oct 1897 CE From Jammu the Swamiji and his party reached Sialkot on Sunday morning. He was warmly received and taken to accommodation arranged in the residence of Lala Mulchand, a pleader. (LSV 2.287)
2 31 Oct 1897 Lecture That evening he spoke on "Religion" in English. After the lecture he gave a summary of it in Hindi for those who did not know English. (LSV 2.287)
3 1 Nov 1897 I Swamiji was interviewed by prominent people of Sialkot including two woman sannyasinis and He expressed his will to start school for girls. (LSV 2. 287)
4 Nov 1897 Lecture Swamiji's lectured on BHAKTI at Sialkote in hindi (CW SV 3.357 LSV 2.288)
5 3 Nov 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to SISTER NIVEDITA From JAMMU (CW SV 8.434)
6 5 Nov 1897 S On Friday afternoon Swamiji and party arrived in Lahore. He was welcomed at the station by residents of the city, and taken to the palace of Raja Dhyan Singh. Conversed with visitors. (LSV 2.288)
7 5 Nov 1897 0 After his meal Swamiji went to stay at the house of Shri Nagendranath Gupta, Editor of the Tribune. (LSV 2.288)
8 Nov 1897 0 The Arya Samajists gave him a welcome. Lala Hansaraj, President of the Dayananda Anglo-Vedic College, often had talks with the Swami. (LSV 2.288)
9 Nov 1897 0 Daily, in the morning for two hours and in the afternoon for an hour and a half, about two hundred Bengali and Punjabi residents of Lahore would gather at the palace of Raja Dhyan Singh to meet the Swami and discuss religious matters. (LSV 2.288)
10 Nov 1897 I One day he was praising a certain person at length. On hearing it all, one of those with him said, "But Swamiji, that gentleman has no respect for you!" The Swami at once replied, "Is it necessary to respect me in order to become a good man?" The questioner was taken aback at these words. (LSV 2.288)

12 5 Nov 1897 Lecture In the evening Swamiji lectured on " The problems before us" (THE COMMON BASES OF HINDUISM) in large court yard of Old Palace at Lahore (CW SV 3.366 LSV 2. 288)
13 Nov 1897 S At Lahore met Mr Tirtha Ram goswami who later took sanyasa with name Rama Tirtha and preached Vedanta in India and America. (LSV 2.291)
14 Nov 1897 I One evening the Swami, accompanied by his brother-monks, Tirtha Ram, and a number of young men, was walking along the road. The party broke into several groups. "In the last group," according to Swami Ram Tirtha, in a letter written at a later date from Darjeeling, "in answer to a question, I was explaining: 'An ideal Mahatma is one who has lost all sense of separate personality and lives as the Self of all. When the air in any region absorbs enough of the solar heat, it becomes rarefied and rises higher. The air from different regions then rushes in to occupy this vacuum, thus setting the whole atmosphere in motion. So does a Mahatma marvellously infuse life and spirit into a nation through self-reform.' The Swami's group happening to be silent at the time, he overheard this part of our conversation and stopped suddenly and emphatically remarked, 'Such was my guru, Paramahamsa Ramakrishna Deva.'" (LSV 2.292)
15 Nov 1897 I Motilal Bose, a boyhood neighbour and playmate of the Swamiji, and now the owner of Professor Bose's Circus, came to meet him. (LSV 2.294)
16 9 Nov 1897 Lecture Swamiji's lecture BHAKTI at Lahore in hindi (CW SV 3.385 LSV 2.288)
17 11 Nov 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From LAHORE (CW SV 8.435)
18 12 Nov 1897 Lecture Swamiji's lecture THE VEDANTA at Lahore (CW SV 3.393 LSV 2.288)
19 14 Nov 1897 S An open-air party was given in honour of the Swmiji on Sunday evening, on the lawn of Lahore Town Hall. It was attended by many prominent people. (LSV 2. 290)
20 Nov 1897 S Swamiji invited by the Sikhs of the "Shuddhi Sabha". He appreciated their work of accepting back into the Sikh faith those Sikhs who had been converted to Islam. (LSV 2. 290)
21 Nov 1897 S The Swami's non-sectarian outlook was especially evident at Lahore. (LSV 2. 290)
22 15 Nov 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to MITRA INDUMATI From LAHORE (CW SV 6.413)
23 15 Nov 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From LAHORE (CW SV 8.436)
24 Nov 1897 S Mission in Punjab was pre eminently to establish peace and harmony among arya samajist- who stood for reinterpreted hinduism and sanatanis - who were for orthodox hinduism (LSV 2.291)
25 1897 I The relationship between the Swami and Tirtha Ram was most amicable, and the lat-ter presented the Swami with a gold watch before he left. The Swami kindly accepted it, but put it back in Tirtha Ram's pocket, saying, "Very well, friend, I shall wear it here, in this pocket." (LSV 2.292)
26 16 Nov 1897 Letter Ram Tirtha's letter to Pandit Din Dayal Vyakhyana Vachaspati on Swamiji's visit to Lahore. (LSV 2.293)
27 Nov 1897 I A touching incident occurred at Lahore. (LSV 2.293)
28 1897 CE It was chiefly the state of the Swamiji's health that made him leave Lahore for Dehra Dun, after ten days of strenuous work. The return to the plains had caused a relapse of the illness that had taken him to the Himalayas, and he was consequently forced to postpone the completion of his lecture tour. (LSV 2.294)
