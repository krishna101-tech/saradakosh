New York: saratoga (Sept 1893)

1 4 Sep 1893 CE Swamiji arrived In Saratoga at invitation of Mr. Franklin Sanborn and stayed at boarding house called Sanatorium. (ND 1.053)
2 4 Sep 1893 S Swamiji to speak before the convention of the American Social Science Association of which Mr. F.B. Sanborn was the secretary (LSV 1.410 ND 1.055)
3 5 Sep 1893 News Report News Report on Swamiji's lecture at the East Church in Salem Daily Gazette. (LSV 3.469 ND 1.051)
4 5 Sep 1893 Lecture Swamiji's lecture "The Mohammedan Rule in India" before American Social Science Association on 5th evening. (LSV 1.410 ND 1.055)
5 6 Sep 1893 News Report News Report on Swamiji at Saratoga in 'Daily Saratogian'. (CW SV 3.470 ND 1.056)
6 6 Sep 1893 Lecture Swamiji's lecture "The Use of Silver in India" before American Social Science Association on 6th morning. (LSV 1.410 ND 1.055)
7 6 Sep 1893 Lecture Swamiji's lecture before American Social Science Association on 6th evening. The title of this talk is not known. (LSV 1.410 ND 1.055)
8 Sep 1893 S Swamiji did not mention about these lectures in his letters. (LSV 1.410 ND 1.055)
9 Sep 1893 S Unfortunately, the text of none of his talks was reported upon. Nevertheless, the newspaper articles which carried accounts of his appearances at the convention and at "Dr. Hamilton's" are reproduced here in part. (ND 1.055)
10 7 Sep 1893 News Report News Report on Swamiji at Saratoga in 'Daily Saratogian'. (ND 1.057)
11 Sep 1893 S In addition Swamiji spoke twice to crowded parlors at Dr. Hamilton's on the manners customs and beliefs of the people of India. (ND 1.055)
12 Sep 1893 S Three weeks stay in East Coast gave opportunity to Swamiji to prepare for all that was to come. He was also equipped with all credentials necessary at parliament of Religions. (LSV 1.411 ND 1.058)
13 Sep 1893 S In the Saratogian there ran an advertisement headed "Half Rate to the World's Fair," which recommended "the excellent Chicago train service on the Delaware and Hudson Rail¬road. . . . $26.00 round trip. (ND 1.058)
14 Sep 1893 S If Swamiji took advantage of this bargain, he would have arrived in Chicago at the handsome red brick Dearborn Street Station on the corner of Dearborn and Polk streets on the evening of either Friday, September 8, or Saturday, September 9. (ND 1.058)
15 7 Sep 1893 CE Probably Swamiji left by train ' Delaware and Hudson ' for Chicago. (ND 1.058)
