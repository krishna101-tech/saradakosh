Guru and Disciple

1 S The relationship of Shri Ramakrishna and Vivekananda was a spiritual relationship without a touch of worldliness. (LSV 1.084)
2 S Sri Ramakrishna wanted to impart the truth to Naren, but he needed a time to ripen. Sri Ramakrishna tells him a story of evil spirit anxious to have a companion. (LSV 1.084)
3 S Shri Ramakrishna's love for Naren was so deep that if Naren failed to come to Dakshineswar for some days he would become disconsolate. He would weep and pray to the Divine Mother, begging Her to make him come, and refusing to be comforted in the meantime. (LSV 1.085)
4 S Naren said at that time, "It is his love for me that binds me to him." (LSV 1.085)
5 S Shri Ramakrishna was much disturbed when Narendra did not appear at Dakshineswar for several days. He was restlessly requesting two devotees to persuade Naren to come. (LSV 1.085)
6 S Another disciple describes the restlessness of sri Ramakrishna on account of the prolonged absence of Narendranath. (LSV 1.085)
7 S But Sri Ramakrishna's joy was correspondingly great when Naren came. (LSV 1.086)
8 S By mere touch of Naren, Sri Ramakrishna used to fall in to deep Samadhi. (LSV 1.086)
9 S Narendra used to go to see Sri Ramakrishna once or twice a week but subsequently family trouble prevented him. Sri Ramakrishna used to console himself during those days. (LSV 1.086)
10 S Sri Ramakrishna had tremendos faith in Naren, while ordinary people use to critisize many aspects of Naren's nature. (LSV 1.086)
11 S Sri Ramakrishna thought so highly of Naren that if anybody spoke disparagingly of Naren, Sri Ramakrishna use to consider that as Shiv-ninda. (LSV 1.087)
12 I Sri Ramakrishna comparing Naren with Keshab Chandra Sen and Vijay krishna Goswami said to some devotees that Naren is like a blazing sun while they are like a candle-flame. Naren Protested to the Master. (LSV 1.087)
13 I Naren escorted Sri Ramakrishna from Brahmo Samaj Service in Calcutta to Dakshineswar because of commotion created on account of later's presence. (LSV1.089)
14 I Naren felt pain to see ill-treatment to Sri Ramakrishna on his account, uses harsh words to Shri Ramakrishna about his blind love. But Shri Ramakrishna dismissed all, saying 'he sees God in Naren' (LSV1.089)
15 I Sri Ramakrishna asks Kali Mother about his relationship with Naren and other young boys and gets reply. (LSV1.090)
16 S Sri Ramakrishna gets comforts from Bholanath's answer for his relationship with Naren and other boys. (LSV1.090)
17 S Shri Ramakrishna himself explained on various occasions why Naren was so dear to him. (LSV1.091)
18 S Once Sri Ramakrishna went to Naren's house to see him. Asked him to sing and became unconscious of all outward things. (LSV1.092)
19 S On one of Naren's early visits to Dakshineswar,Shri Ramakrishna said to him, "Look! In you is Shiva! In me is Shakti! And these two are One!" (LSV1.092)
20 S Shri Ramakrishna's relationship with Narendra differed a great deal from that with the other disciples. He expected them to observe restrictions as regards food, meditation, prayer, sleep and other affairs of daily life. There were no such restrictions for Naren. Sri Ramakrishna had reasons for this. (LSV1.093)
21 S Narendra wondered at discrimination exercised by sri Ramakrishna as to food and the receiving of presents from certain persons. He determined to find out for himself. He found that in every case Shri Ramakrishna had been right. (LSV1.093)
22 S Naren useed to enter in arguments with other devotees. Sri Ramakrishna was delighted when he found others much older than Naren unable to withstand Naren's reasoning power. (LSV1.094)
23 S As a member of the Brahmo Samaj, Naren was committed to belief in a formless God. He persuaded Rakhal to embrace Brahmo creed. But at intervation of Sri Ramakrishna he stopped interfering with Rakhal's attitude again. (LSV1.094)
24 S Naren's tendancy to bigotry disappeared at Sri Ramakrishna's argument. On his argument against immage worship, Sri Ramakrishna told him,"before long you will not only acknowledge my beloved Mother, but weep in Her name." (LSV1.094)
25 S Once Naren argued with Sri Ramakrishna on very existance of Radha - Krishna (LSV1.095)
26 S Sri Ramkrishna was glad at heart that Naren was a rebel, because this is required to arrive at illumination. (LSV1.095)
27 S Sri Ramakrishna wanted to initaite Narendra into the truth of the Advaita Vedanta but Narendra, a staunch adherent of the Brahmo Samaj rebeled and did not surrender easily. (LSV1.095)
28 I Sri Ramakrishna persuading his teachings of Advaita Vedanta, initially failed. One day he touched to Narendra and plunged in to samadhi. (LSV1.096)
29 I Naren described the effect of Sri Ramakrishna's touch and at the end he could not deny the conclusions of Advaita Philosophy. (LSV1.096)
30 S Shri Ramakrishna's stepby step teaching and training of Naren resulted into his regarda and acceptance of Shri Ramakrishna as highest ideal of sprituality. (LSV1.097)
31 I Naren testing Shri Ramakrishna for his renunciation of gold. (LSV1.097)
32 I Naren also had to pass many tests before Shri Ramakrishna accepted him. (LSV1.098)
33 I Shri Ramakrishna tested Naren severely for an extended period of about a month. At the end he was highly pleased at the reply given by Naren (LSV1.098)
34 I Shri Ramakrishna wanted to impart his powers to Naren but he refused because he wanted to realize God first. (LSV1.099)
35 S The Master's love for and faith in Naren acted as a restraining force. (LSV1.100)
