New York and Cambridge (Nov - Dec 1894)

1 4 Nov 1894 CE Swamiji back to New York. (ND 2. 224 LSV 1.513)
2 4 Nov 1894 CE Swamiji back to New York unexpectedly. Spend most of the month there and organised the nucleus of VSNY (ND 2. 224 LSV 1.513 LSV 1.514)
3 15 Nov 1894 Letter Swamiji's letter to DESAI HARIDAS BIHARIDAS From CHICAGO (CW SV 8.325)
4 18 Nov 1894 Letter Swamiji's letter to HALE MRS.G W From NEW YORK (CW SV 9.047 ND 2.224)
5 18 Nov 1894 Notes The phonograph Swamiji mentions in the letter had been en route from America to the Maharaja of Khetri for months. (CW SV 9.047 ND 2.225)
6 18 Nov 1894 Writing Swamiji's letter to MUKHERJEE PYARIMOHAN RAJA From NEW YORK (REPLY TO THE CALCUTTA ADDRESS) (CW SV 4.365)
7 19 Nov 1894 Writing Swamiji's letter to ALASINGA PERUMAL From NEW YORK (TO MY BRAVE BOYS) (CW SV 4.367)
8 29 Nov 1894 Letter In reply to Swamiji's letter of October 27 to Alasinga, Madras disciples wrote on November 29, a joint reply. Following excerpts from which show their deep and touching love for their absent Guru. G. G. Narasimhacharya, of whom Swamiji once wrote, "G. G.'s nature is of the emotional type," contributed the first section, Alasinga the second, and Kidi (Singaravelu Mudaliar, a professor of science in Madras) the third: (ND 2.216)
9 30 Nov 1894 CE Whether or not Swamiji lectured in New York during November of 1894, one thing he did do was of great historic significance. Throughout the year he had been scattering the seeds of his Master's message; he now formed a receptacle that would contain and nourish them. (ND 2.226)
10 30 Nov 1894 Letter Swamiji's letter to ALASINGA PERUMAL From USA (CW SV 5.052 ND 2.226)
11 Nov 1894 Notes The purpose of establishing a society Swamiji had in mind at that time was no doubt as much financial as philosophical and religious. It started taking shape. (ND 2.226)

31 Dec 1894 Notes It is probable, though not certain, that among the many classes Swamiji held that December were on Raja Yoga. (ND 2. 231)
32 Dec 1894 Notes From Swamiji's note to Mary Hale it is observed that inspite of his busy schedule at Cambridge he desired to go to Boston to see her as she could not come. (ND 2. 232)
33 Dec 1894 Notes If Swamiji went to Boston to see Mary Hale, it was not until the last week in December when his work lessened to some extent. In the meanwhile, Miss Hale remained distant. "You must have received Mrs. Bull's letter [an invitation] . . . ," Swamiji wrote on December 21. "If you cannot accept it, I shall be very glad to come over any day-I have time now as the lectures are at an end, except Sunday next. (ND 2. 232)

38 27 Dec 1894 Letter At the end of these three weeks Mrs. Bull wrote to Dr. Lewis G. Janes, telling him something of Swamiji's classes and their effect on those who had heard them. (ND 2. 230 LSV 1. 515)
39 Dec 1894 Notes Mrs. Bull took lot of pain to make Swamiji's stay at her house most comfortable and also the tother distinguished guests. (ND 2. 233)
40 Dec 1894 News reports Boston Evening Transcript published an article concerning Mrs. Bull's well attended December Conferences. (ND 2. 235)
41 Dec 1894 Notes During his stay in Cambridge Swamiji became more than casually acquainted not only with his fellow house guests but with the many Harvard professors. (ND 2. 236)
42 Dec 1894 Notes Cambridge Society welcomed Swamiji into their midst without too much alarm. (ND 2. 236)
43 Dec 1894 News reports News Report in Chicago Inter Ocean of December 29. (ND 2. 242)
44 Dec 1894 CE And thus Swamiji's visit in Cambridge drew to a close. Three days later he journeyed to New York to keep an appointment with the officers of the Brooklyn Ethical Association. (ND 2. 243 LSV 1. 516)
45 28 Dec 1894 CE Swamiji left for Brooklyn. (ND 2. 243)
