Punjab: With the Western disciples (June 1898)

-- 1898061400 S Swamiji and party reached Punjab by train. (LSV 2.356)
-- 1898061430 S Passing through Ludhiana, Lahore, and across the Doab, they arrived at Rawalpindi. (LSV 2.356)
-- 1898061500 S From Rawalpindi by tonga to Murree, reaching on June 15, where Swamiji and party stayed for three days. (LSV 2.356)
-- 1898061510 S Sister Nivedita recalls : (LSV 2.357)
Here the Swami came to the conclusion that any effort which he might make to induce the orthodox to accept a European as a fellow-disciple, or in the direction of woman's education, had better be made in Bengal. The distrust of the foreigner was too strong in Punjab, to admit of work succeeding there. He was much occupied by this question, from time to time, and would sometimes remark on the paradox presented by the Bengali combination of political antagonism to the English, and readiness to love and trust.
