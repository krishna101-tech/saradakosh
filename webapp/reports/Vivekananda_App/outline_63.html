Calcutta and Darjeeling (Feb-Apr 1897)

1 Jan 1897 CE The whole of Bengal had been alive with enthusiasm ever since the news flashed that Swami Vivekananda had landed in India. (LSV 2. 215)
2 Jan 1897 CE The Swami too was eagerly looking forward to his return to the city of his birth. (LSV 2. 215)
3 Jan 1897 CE On the ship some American missionaries deplored the Swami’s English disciples’ leaving Christianity for Vedanta. (LSV 2. 216)
4 Jan 1897 CE When the steamer sailed up the Hooghly, the Swami pointed out to his disciples all the places of interest that he knew so well as the places associated with his early youth and manhood. (LSV 2. 216)
5 Jan 1897 CE The Reception Committee in Calcutta had been busy making elaborate preparations for the Swami's arrival following news of his departure from Madras. (LSV 2. 216)

41 Mar 1897 CE Swamiji's triumphs was the conversion of his brother-disciples from an individualistic idea of religious life to one in which concern for others and service to them occupied a prominent place. (LSV 2. 236)

48 Mar 1897 0 (second week) Miss Muller arrived in India (LSV 2.)
49 15 Mar 1897 0 Dr. Barrows returned to US after three months of lecture tour. He preached most bigoted form of Christianity. While lecturing in Calcutta, Madras, poona etc he made no secret of his antipathy to swamiji. After reaching US Barrows started malicising Swamiji, accusing him of defaming american women, bragging his following and unjustified influence in america etc. News papers attacked him on this score. Some american missions, Brahmos, theosophists, Buddhists etc. began to be increasingly vocal in their criticisms of a few hard but true remarks of Swamiji. Swamiji remain indifferent to Barrows and others rantings and replied to a few close american friends in letters (LSV 2.)
