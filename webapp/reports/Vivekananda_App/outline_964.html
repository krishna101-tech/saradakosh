42 1 Jun 1895 Notes Swamiji's first season of New York classes came to a close on the first of June. He was pleased with the work he had done and the response he had received. (ND 3. 092)
43 Jun 1895 CE By the month of June 1895, the Swamiji began to feel himself wearing out. He longed for a brief period, of rest and recuperation. But personally, he was satisfied. (LSV 2. 019)
44 Jun 1895 CE Gradually his disciples came to understand Swamiji's ideal. (LSV 2. 019)
45 Jun 1895 CE Secret of Swamiji's success in impressing the American nation with a new thought. (LSV 2. 019)
46 Jun 1895 CE Swamiji accepted the invitation of Mr. Francis Leggett to his fishing lodge, Camp Percy, beside a lake in the White Mountains of New Hampshire. (LSV 2. 020)
47 Jun 1895 CE Before Swamiji had left for Camp Percy, his students had expressed their eagerness for him to return at once to New York and continue his work of teaching. But being too tired, he had demurred at prolonging his classes through the hot summer months. Many of his students, moreover, had arranged to leave the city for seaside or mountain resorts. (LSV 2. 020)
48 4 Jun 1895 Letter Swamiji's letter to BULL MRS.OLE From NEW YORK (CW SV 9.060 ND 3.094)
49 4 Jun 1895 CE On June 4, 1895, Mr. Francis Leggett’s party of five took a steamer from New York to Portland, Maine, a train from Portland to the small village of Percy, New Hampshire. (ND 3. 104)
50 6 Jun 1895 CE After two days journey Swamiji arrived at Camp percy with Mr. Legget, Betty Sturges, Miss Macleod, Georgia spence. Rested for twelve days. (ND 2. 104)
51 7 Jun 1895 Letter Swamiji's letter to BULL MRS.OLE From HAMPSHIRE, PERCY (CW SV 6.309 LSV 2.020 ND 3. 104)
52 Jun 1895 CE This sojourn at Camp Percy was a sort of betrothal party in the wilds. Besse (or Betty) MacLeod Sturges and Francis Leggett had just become engaged and were planning to be married in Paris in September. (ND 3. 105)
