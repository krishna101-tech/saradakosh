London (July-aug 1899)

1 31 Jul 1899 Notes Swamiji reaches London. Christine and Mrs Funke received him at dock, who travelled from America to London to receive him. It being summer time most of Swamiji's friends and disciples were out of town. (ND 5.052 LSV 2. 477)
2 Jul 1899 Letter Of this event Nivedita wrote to Miss MacLeod, "We arrived on Monday morning [July 31]. (ND 5.067)
3 Jul 1899 CE The story of tragedy of Mr. Sturdy. (From March 1895 that Mr. Sturdy introduced himself by aletter to Swamiji to December 1896, Swamiji sailed back for India). (ND 5.052)
4 Jul 1899 CE The story of tragedy of Mr. Sturdy while Swamiji was in India. (ND 5.056)
5 Jul 1899 CE Rest of the story of tragedy of Mr. Sturdy. (ND 5.060)
6 1899 S Sturdy avoided Swamiji's company. He was unsatisfied with the latter's way of life. His devotion to the cause was gone sour. Though he didn’t react in presence of Swamiji, later on he engage in acrimonious correspondence with the latter. His defection and Miss Muller's malicious gossips alienated many of Swamiji's London friends. Thus during his stay at Wimbeldon few of his old acquintances came to meet him (LSV 2.478 ND 5.063)
7 1899 S In I899 Wimbledon was a charming English town, full of trees, history, old houses, curving roads, and a large wooded common where one could take long meandering walks. (ND 5.069)
8 1899 S Inspite of the seeming collapse of London work, the rest at Wimbeldon and the company of friends relaxed Swamiji and he recovered a lot (LSV 2.479 ND)
9 1899 S It was during this period that the members of the Noble household came in intimate contact with the Swami for the first time. (LSV 2.479 ND)
