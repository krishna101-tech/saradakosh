Ocean voyage (June 1899)

1 Jun 1899 Writing MEMOIRS OF EUROPEAN TRAVEL (CW SV 7.297)
2 20 Jun 1899 CE Swamiji boarded the S.S. Golconda at the Calcutta port on the Hooghly River and was off for the West, accompanied by Swami Turiyananda and Sister Nivedita and began his second and last voyage to the Western world. (ND 5.001 LSV 2.458)
3 21 Jun 1899 Letter Swamiji's letter to MACLEOD JOSEFINE From CALCUTTA (CW SV 9.116)
4 1899 S Background to Swamiji's second visit to the West (ND 5.001)

6 Jun 1899 CE Swamiji’s brother Bhupendranath Datta wrote many years later in his book Swami Vivekananda: Patriot Prophet. (ND 5.019)
7 Jun 1899 CE Initial riding was of exceedingly rough seas for more than two days and nights in the Bay of Bengal, (ND 5.021 LSV 2.458)
8 Jun 1899 CE Yet the turbulent blue sea into whichthe Golconda had entered was, in a sense, still a part of the “Land of Renunciation,” for the sacred soil of India literally carpeted its floor. (ND 5.021)
9 24 Jun 1899 CE Reached Madras at night. Were not allowed to land on shore as the ship was under quarantine due to the plague in Calcutta. All Madrasi devotees gathered on the shore to greet him. (ND 5. 024 LSV 2. 458)
10 24 Jun 1899 CE From one point of view, the regulation that kept him aboard the Golconda was heaven-sent. (ND 5. 025)

13 Jun 1899 CE Swamiji apparently did not suffer from seasickness; nor did his Madrasi disciple Alasinga Perumal, who had hurriedly bought a ticket to Colombo and boarded the ship. (ND 5.027)
