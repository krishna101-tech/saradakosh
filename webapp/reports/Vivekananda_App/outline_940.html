With Thakur at Kashipur (Dec 1885 - Aug 1886)

1 11 Dec 1885 CE Thakur moved to Kashipur. Mother goes along with him. Naren and other young men started living with him serving him whole heartedly. (KTMGMLSV 1.158)
2 Dec 1885 CE The move to Kashipur ushered in the last scene of the Shri Ramakrishna's life. These were days of intense physical suffering for him. (LSV 1.158)
3 Dec 1885 CE Shri Ramakrishna grew physically worse.was again ordered by his physician not to strain his throat by talking; but he did not resist the urge to transmit his knowledge to the many religious seekers. (LSV 1.158)
4 Dec 1885 CE Under direction of Naren, brother disciples stay day and night at Kashipur garden. Relatives opposed. Naren was studying for Law examination in time left to him at Kashipur. (LSV 1.158)
5 1885 CE Naren was a constant source of inspiration to brother disciples. They were twelve in number, every one of them a tower of strength in consecration and single minded devotion. (LSV 1.159)
6 1885 S One night Naren and few brother disciples were in the garden and light Dhuni fires to burn their desires. (LSV 1.159)
7 1885 I One day Shri Ramakrishna initiated Naren with a Mantra of Rama. Towards evening Naren began to circle the house, repeating the name of the Lord, "Rama! Rama!", in a loud and animated voice. Outward consciousness had apparently left him, and he was full of ecstatic fire. (LSV 1.160)
8 1885 S The Kashipur garden house became a temple and a university hall in one. (LSV 1.160)
9 1 Jan 1886 I The incident occurred today made Shri Ramakrishna the fabled Wish fulfilling Tree (Kalpataru) and granted devotees spiritual realizations according to their path and capacity. In memory of this incident, Kalpataru day is celebrated every year on January 1 at the kashipur garden and elsewhere. (LSV 1.160)
10 4 Jan 1886 0 Naren's passion for God (KTM 2.935)
11 4 Jan 1886 I One day (January 4, 1886) the Master asked Naren, "Won't you continue your college studies?" With emotion the latter replied, "Sir, I shall feel greatly relieved if I find some medicine that will make me forget all I have studied." (LSV 1.161)
12 4 Jan 1886 I The conversation of Naren with Mahendranath Gupta reavels Naren's frame of mind. (LSV 1.161)
13 4 Jan 1886 S Shri Ramakrishna gave a hint to Niranjan and Shashi that Naren was soon to reach the goal. (LSV 1.162)
14 1886 S That very night Naren with some of his brother-disciples left for Dakshineswar to practise meditation. (LSV 1.163)
15 1886 S There was nothing unnatural in Naren's entreaty for Samadhi. But Shri Ramakrishna was fully aware that he would enable Naren to realize a higher and nobler state than Samadhi. (LSV 1.163)
16 1886 S Naren attained remarkable results in carrying out Master's instructions in meditation. (LSV 1.163)
17 1886 S Shri Ramakrishna instructed Naren to take care of brother disciples. (LSV 1.163)
18 1886 I One day, in preparation for the monastic life, the Master instructed the boys to beg their food from door to door. (LSV 1.163)
19 1886 S Naren was looked up to by the other young disciples because of Shri Ramakrishna's estimate of his spiritual worth. (LSV 1.164)
20 1886 S Shri Ramakrishna encouraged this central position of Naren in innumerable ways. (LSV 1.164)
21 1886 S In the midst of all his striving and hankering for the realization of Truth,. Naren did not lose sight of the fact that it was through the grace of the Master that he was being prepared for the realization of God. The illness of the Master-his friend, philosopher and Guru-was constantly in his mind. (LSV 1.164)
22 1886 I A great scholar and a devotee of Ramakrishna, came to Kashipur advising Shri Ramakrishna to cure his sickness by a wish. Shri ramakrishna's reply silanced the pandit. (LSV 1.164)
23 1886 I Naren and brother disciples also persuaded the Master to beg the devine Mother and cure himself for the sake of disciples. Master did it and got the reply from devine Mother. (LSV 1.165)
24 1886 S Naren was startled at these words. What an absence of body consciousness! What a firm realization of the Truth of Advaita! Naren knew then that the Master was unique in his realizations. (LSV 1.165)
25 1886 I According to Shri Ramakrishna, Naren entered in the higher stages of meditation. (LSV 1.165)
26 1886 S Naren was now becoming aware of the spiritual power within him. There were moments when he, as it were, touched divinity and became almost physically conscious of Reality by the spiritual transmutation of the internal faculties of sense. His thought became a sweeping power. (LSV 1.166)
27 5 Jan 1886 0 With Naren etc (KTM 2.937)
28 Feb 1886 0 (evening) Thakur wrote on a piece of paper "Naren will teach" (ARR)
29 Mar 1886 I Naren displayed his spritual power on Shivaratri. (LSV 1.166)
30 Mar 1886 I Shri Ramakrishna scolded Naren on the above incident telling him, "This is the time to gain power, not to spend it". (LSV 1.167)
31 Mar 1886 S Shri Ramakrishna Was sinking daily. The anxiety and grief of the devotees knew no bounds. (LSV 1.167)
32 11 Mar 1886 0 With Naren, Shashi, Buro gopal, Sarat etc (KTM 2.939)
33 14 Mar 1886 0 With Naren, Rakhal, Girish etc (KTM 2.940)
34 15 Mar 1886 I Shri Ramakrishna felt better and talked to the devotees. (LSV 1.168)
35 15 Mar 1886 0 With Naren, Rakhal, Latu etc (KTM 2.941)
36 15 Mar 1886 I Shri Ramakrishna went into Samadhi. He regained partial consciousness of the world. Long conversation between Shri ramakrishna and his dvotees took place. (LSV 1.168)
37 Apr 1886 S The chief topic of discussion and meditation among the disciples had been the life and gospel of the Buddha.Naren, kali and Tarak left for Buddha Gaya. (LSV 1.172)
38 Apr 1886 S Naren and brother disciples reached gaya and set in meditation under Bodhi Tree. Naren had vision and could not control his feelings. (LSV 1.173)
39 Apr 1886 S Brother disciples at Kashipur were anxious about Naren. Master assured them about Naren's return. (LSV 1.173)
40 Apr 1886 0 Naren visits Bodh-gaya along with shivananda and kali without informing anybody (KM)
41 Apr 1886 S Shri Ramakrishna assured disciples at Kashipur that Naren will not go beyond Bodhi Gaya and will return. It truly happened and they returned shortly. (LSV 1.173)
42 Apr 1886 0 (Apr end) The next day after they returned, Thakur wrote on a piece of paper "give knowledge to Narendra" (ARR)
43 9 Apr 1886 S Shri Ramakrishna had discussions with Naren and Mahendra and others on Buddha. (LSV 1.174)
44 9 Apr 1886 0 With Naren, Kali, Niranjan, Shashi, Rakhal etc (KTM 2.947)
45 1886 S Shri Ramakrishna initiated the boys into monastic life. (LSV 1.176)
46 1886 S Naren wanted to realize the central truth of the Upanishads,"Aham Brahmasmi", "I am Brahman". (LSV 1.177)
47 1886 I One evening Naren had experience of realization. (LSV 1.177)
48 1886 I Naren experienced the Absolute Realization (Nirvikalpa Samadhi) (LSV 1.178)
49 1886 I It was Naren's intense desire to realize the absolute Brahman that decided Shri Ramakrishna to give it to him, but the Master had no intention of permitting him to stay there. (LSV 1.179)
50 1886 S There were three other occassions on which Narendranath experienced Nirvikalpa Samadhi (LSV 1.179)
51 1886 S Naren remembered Master spent four years in his spritual training. (LSV 1.180)
52 1886 S The days passed in devotion, in service, in sorrow, in ecstasy Naren leading, the others following. (LSV 1.179)
53 1886 I An unpleasant, but significant incident occurred at Kashipur which shows how much Shri Ramakrishna loved and trusted Narendranath. (LSV 1.180)
