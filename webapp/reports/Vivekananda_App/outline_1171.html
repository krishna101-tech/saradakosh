Massachusets: Annisquam (Aug 1893)

1 25 Aug 1893 CE Swamiji came to Annesquam at invitation of prof. John.H. Wright. (LSV 1.405 ND 1.027)
2 25 Aug 1893 S Professor Wright was at once appreciative of Swamiji's genius and persuaded him of the importance of attending the Parliament of Religions. (ND 1.019)
3 25 Aug 1893 S Prof Wright wrote to the Chairman, of the Committee for the Selection of Delegates, giving him swamiji's credentials (LSV 1.405 ND 1.027)
4 25 Aug 1893 S Swamiji stayed as Professor Wright’s guest at Miss Lane’s boarding house (ND 1.064)
5 25 Aug 1893 S Boarders at Miss. Lane's boarding house and Annisquam villager's first glimpse of Swamiji. (ND 1.030 LSV 1.406)
6 25 Aug 1893 S Actually, as one learns from the above entry in Mrs. Wright's more accurate diary, it was not to the Wright's tiny cottage that the party repaired after supper, but to that of Professor Eugene Wambaugh of the Harvard Law School, who, with his family, was summering at Annisquam. It was there that Swamiji enthralled and startled the little gathering. (ND 1.031)
7 25 Aug 1893 Lecture Swamiji had an eveninng talk in Annisquam at home of Professor Wambaugh, Harvard University Law School. Recorded by MRS. WRIGHT as "VENGEANCE OF HISTORY." (CW SV 7.278 ND 1.031)
8 Aug 1893 S Swamiji made prophetic utterances about China and Russia in Annisquam.- Reported by sister Christine in '"Reminiscences" (ND 1.035)
9 25 Aug 1893 News Report News Report on Swamiji in Boston Evening transcript (ND 1.025)
10 26 Aug 1893 S Swamiji used to talk all morning and afternoon. Yet he also played with the Wright's children. (ND 1.035)
11 Aug 1893 S Swamiji's affection for Prof. Wright's son Austin. (ND 1.036)
12 Aug 1893 S Austin Wright invented an imaginary place 'Islandia' . The invention of this novel started when Swamiji visited Annisquam. And finally ran into 2300 pages at the time of death of Austin in 1941. (ND 1.036)
13 26 Aug 1893 S Swamiji had a visit and talk at home of profeesor Alpheus Hyatt, the famous zoologist. (ND 1.039)
14 27 Aug 1893 S The "revival" in the Wright household continued in full force on Sunday also, when Swamiji "talked religion" during the morning and afternoon. (ND 1.039)
15 27 Aug 1893 S In the evening Swamiji gave his first public lecture at Annisquam church. (ND 1.039 LSV 1.406)
16 27 Aug 1893 S A woman recollects swamiji saying in the Annisquam village Church that the Hindus were taught to have great respect for other people's religions (ND 1.040)
17 Aug 1893 S Here is a vivid description of the Swamiji in these early days, written by the professor's wife. It is taken from a long sketch Mrs. Wright wrote, using notes that she had made during the Swamiji's brief visit: (LSV 1.406)
18 Aug 1893 CE Aside from its importance in opening Swamiji's way to the Parliament of Religions, this weekend lastingly enriched the Wright family as well it might have, for none fortunate enough to have Swamiji as a guest soon forgot him. (ND 1.028)
19 Aug 1893 CE The memory of swamiji's visit to Annesquam and later meetings became a part of the Wright family tradition. (ND 1.029)
20 28 Aug 1893 News Report News Report on Swamiji in Gloucester Daily Times (ND 1.039)
21 28 Aug 1893 S Swamiji departed Annisquam for Salem. On that day and next day, the weather at Annisquam was rainy and cyclonic. Swamiji later came to be called The "cyclonic monk". (ND 1.040 LSV 1.410)
