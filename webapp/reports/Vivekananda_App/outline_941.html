Merath (Dec 1890)

1 Nov 1890 S Swamiji with brother- disciples went to saharanpur and then to Meerut to meet sick Gangadhar mj. (LSV 1.258)
2 Nov 1890 S Gangadhar Mj was happy to see Swmiji but was sad about his health. Swamiji stayed with him at doctor's house, while others stayed at shethji's garden house. (LSV 1.258)
3 Dec 1890 S All arrived together to meet sick Swami Gangadhar mj at Merath. Budo gopal arrived there on his way to pilgrimage (LSV 1.259)
4 Dec 1890 I Swamiji astonished Librarian and Gangadhar Mj about his speed of reading and his memory power (LSV 1.259)
5 Dec 1890 S Many Bengali gentlemen and others also came to the garden to hear religious discourse from the Swami's lips. (LSV 1.260)
6 Dec 1890 I Swamiji remembers incident of free, stern life of the asceticsin the Hardwar and Hrishikesh (LSV 1.260)
7 Dec 1890 CE Such a life was calling the Swamiji, if not in all the severity of its outward form, at least in its spirit, and in its desire for realization and for solitude. His longing for Self realization became so great that his brother-disciples were struck with awe. (LSV 1.260)
8 Dec 1890 S At Meerut the Swami told them that he had decided on the immediate course he was going to follow, and that he already knew his mission. He had received the command of God re-garding his future, and was going to leave them in order to become a solitary monk. (LSV 1.261)
9 Dec 1890 CE Swamiji had a conversation with Gangadhar Mj on this matter (LSV 1.261)
10 Jan 1891 I And so, one morning in the latter part of January 1891, the Swami left his devoted brethren and journeyed on to Delhi by himself. (LSV 1.261)
