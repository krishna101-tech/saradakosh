Chicago (May-June 1894)

1 24 May 1894 CE Swamiji arrived in Chicago on May 24 and remained at the Hale's through most of June, getting rest. (ND 2.077 LSV 1.478)
2 May 1894 CE During month of June 1894, Swamiji found time to write of his plans for the rejuvenation of his moherland. (ND 2.079)
3 May 1894 CE The news of the unparalleled success of the Swami in America began to reach India after about two months after the great Parliament of Religions had commenced in Chicago. (LSV 1.478)
4 May 1894 CE Though people in India were jubilant, it did not occur to them to take united and official action to acknowledge Swamiji publicly as the champion of their country and to thank the American people for their reception of him. (LSV 1.479 ND 2.079)
5 May 1894 CE From the day of the Swami's extraordinary and spectacular success at the Parliament of Religions, American public was thrilled. (LSV 1.479)
6 May 1894 CE Ever since the opening of the Parliament of Religions, the Hindu communities in India had uttered not a word of official support, making situation difficult for Swamiji in America. (ND 2.079 LSV 1.480)
7 May 1894 CE Swamiji, however, received no answer to his important letter to Alasinga from New York on 9th April till the second week of July. (ND 2.080 LSV 1.485)

54 Jun 1894 CE The actual letters and resolutions of the Madras Meeting did not reach America until almost another two months had passed; and thus the suspense went on. The whole thing, from start to finish, was abhorrent to Swamiji. The anxiety, the necessity for "a boom," the need to prove himself, the endless collecting of newspaper clippings-all this was, he felt, a blotch on that unsullied "disregard of gain or name or fame" with which, in actual fact, he worked. (ND 2.105)
