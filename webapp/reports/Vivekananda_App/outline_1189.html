Final Visit to Detroit (23 Mar - 31 Mar 1894)

1 23 Mar 1894 CE Swamiji returned to Detroit [23 to 29] (CB SV)
2 Mar 1894 News reports As per the announcement in Sunday News Tribune of March 18, Swamiji was prevailed upon to deliver a lecture on March 24 in the unitarian church in Detroit. (ND 1.443)
3 Mar 1894 CE At this time Swamiji met Mrs. M'cDuvel so brainy so spiritual and so spiritual to unwordly. (ND 1.278)
4 Mar 1893 CE Swamiji probably met Mrs. Phelps at this time (ND 3.331-32)
5 24 Mar 1894 News Reports Swamiji evening Lecture at Detroit Unitarian Church- 'WOMEN OF INDIA' silenced criticisms from missionaries vested in "converting and civilizing" India. (CW SV 3.505, ND 1.443)
6 25 Mar 1894 News reports Unfortunately his lecture on "The Women of India" was reported upon only briefly by the Free Press and the Evening News of March 25, respectively, as follows. (An item in the Journal was a repetition of that in the Free Press.) (ND 1.444)
7 Mar 1894 Notes Swamiji did not say everything in this lecture that he had said in his afternoon talks on the same subject. Fortunately, however, someone had the good judgment to take notes at one of those informal gatherings, and thus at least some of the things that Swamiji did not repeat in the lecture were preserved for the Detroit public (and for us) (ND 1. 445)
8 Mar 1894 Notes Swamiji's talk at small gatherings were noted by some one. Appeared in the Tribune of April 1, 1894 under following titles: Women of India, India's First Inhebitants, A Clip at American Men, Burnings Compared, Mothers are Sacred and Other Thoughts: (ND 1.445)
9 Mar 1894 CE "The Women of India" was Swamiji's last lecture in the "dynamic city." He wanted now to move on, away from the Midwest. (ND 1.445)
10 25 Mar 1893 Notes Speculations about Swamiji's collections for India during his mid western lecture tour. (ND 1.433)
11 25 Mar 1893 News reports Following item in the Detroit Critic, March 25, pointed out how little he was actually making: (ND 1.434)
12 25 Mar 1893 News reports News Report in Detroit Critic of March 25. (ND 1.436)
13 27 Mar 1894 Letter Swamiji's letter to HALE MRS.G W From DETROIT (CW SV 9.015 ND 1.449)
14 30 Mar 1894 Letter Swamiji's letter to HALE MARY From DETROIT (CW SV 8.304 ND 1.449 LSV 1.471)
15 Mar 1894 CE Thus, just as Swamiji was about to move on to the East Coast invitations from that area came to him unbidden. (ND 1.450)
16 Mar 1894 CE Swamiji's lecture 'Christian Missions in India' silenced the opposition. (ND 1.450)
17 Mar 1894 CE As far as America was concerned, the opposition went underground. (ND 1.450)
18 Mar 1894 Notes A story told by Swamiji to Swami Vijnanananda about a plot by enemies to do away with him altogether. (ND 1.451)
19 Mar 1894 CE "But the Guru is with me," he had written in another connection to Swami Ramakrishnananda in January of 1894, "what could anyone do?" (ND 1.451)
20 Mar 1894 S Swamiji's friends were also unshakably with him. (ND 1.451)
21 Mar 1894 S Romain Rolland also writes in biography of Swamiji. (ND 1.451)
22 Mar 1894 S According to an early copy, the following letter was addressed to a Mrs. Smith; which Mrs. Smith she was is not clear at present, but she evidently knew Swamiji (though not well) and troubled by gossipers, had sought a statement from Mrs. Bagley that would settle the matter of his probity once and for all and thereby silence his detractors. (ND 1.451)
