New York: Vedanta for the World III (Nov 1895 - Dec 1895)

1 27 Nov 1895 CE Swamiji on his way to New York. The S. S. Brittanic was by no means the most modern "luxury liner" afloat. (ND 3.313)
2 28 Nov 1895 Letter Swamiji's letter to STURDY E T From steamer 'BRITTANIC' on way to New York. (CW SV 8.358)
3 5 Dec 1895 Letter Swamiji's letter to STURGES ALBERTA From steamer 'BRITTANIC' on his way to New York. (CW SV 6.351)
4 Dec 1895 CE During Swami Vivekananda's absence from America of nearly four months, his work had continued as best it could without him, carried on and even spread to some extent by his disciples. (LSV 2.057)
5 Dec 1895 CE Swaniji's American disciples Madame Louise and Leon Landsberg tried in their own enthusiastic ways to keep the message of Vedanta a living force until the Swami returned. (LSV 2.057 ND 3.318)
6 Dec 1895 Letter Swami Kripananda's work on Swmiji's mission is revealed by various letters written by him to Mrs. Bull. (ND 3.318)
7 Dec 1895 Letter Meanwhile, Swami Abayananda had also worked earnestly during Swamiji's absence in an effort to spread his teachings and to make disciples. This is also revealed from her letters to Mrs. Bull. (ND 3.323)
8 Dec 1895 Letter Abhayananda had complained to Swamiji of her difficulties and in addition, evidently expressed an intention, or threat, to defect. Swamiji had replied from England to Abhayananda in October of 1895. (ND 3.325)
9 Dec 1895 CE But be that as it may, it is clear enough that Swami Kripananda and Swami Abhayananda had done everything in their power to propagate Swamiji's message while he was away. (ND 3.329)
10 6 Dec 1895 CE Swamiji returns to New York. Stayed with Landsberg (kripananda) at 228 west 29th st. lodging quarter rented for both. (LSV 2.058 ND 3.313)
11 Dec 1895 Letter Swamiji's letter to STURDY E T From NEW YORK (CW SV 8.361)
12 6 Dec 1895 CE Swamiji's stayed with his disciple Kripananda at the lodging house at at 228 West Thirty¬ninth Street. (ND 3.314)
13 Dec 1895 News reports Swamiji's arrival was, infact, viewed with amazement by at least one reporter, who hastened to interview this extraordinay visitor. 14 Dec 1895 CE Before starting his work, Swamiji had the weekend to recover from his days of illness at sea and to find his land legs once again. He also spend time in meeting people. (ND 3.329)
15 Dec 1895 CE As soon as possible Swamiji began his classes, eager, it would seem, to plunge into this second season of his New York work. (ND 3.330)

20 9 Dec 1895 CE Swamiji holds vedanta classes again at his lodgings till X- mas holidays. The schedule for December was made. Even as Swamiji’s schedule settled into a pattern, so also did the “household.” (LSV ND 3.333)
21 Dec 1895 CE As Sister Devamata (Laura Glenn in the 1890s) tells the story in her "Memories of India and Indians", Miss Sarah Ellan Waldo's service was continuous and untiring. She cooked, edited, cleaned, and took dictation, taught and managed, read proof and saw visitors." (ND 3.333)

25 11 Dec 1895 Lecture Swamiji's class lecture on THE FIRST STEPS TOWARDS JNANA delivered in New York, Wednesday; recorded by Swami Kripananda/ Leon Landsberg. (CW SV 9.208 ND 3.358-63)
26 Dec 1895 S Swamiji continued with the further prerequisites of jnana yoga: the six steps of sensory and mental control; intense desire for freedom; and discrimination between the Real and the unreal. (ND 3.363)
27 Dec 1895 Lecture Swamiji's Lecture "THE FIRST STEP TOWARDS JNANA" took down by Kripananda. (ND 3.358)

30 Dec 1895 S Mr. Josiah J. Goodwin was engaged by Vedant Society New York to record Samiji's lectures in shorthand. (LSV 2.058 ND 3.335)
31 Dec 1895 S Swamiji's impact upon the young Goodwin was profound and evidently immediate. (ND 3.338)
32 Dec 1895 S Goodwin forthwith plunged heart and soul into the Vedanta movement. (ND 3.338)
33 Dec 1895 S Later Kripananda was to write to Mrs. Bull on December 20 about his views on the "household". (ND 3.339)
34 Dec 1895 News reports The information regarding Swamiji's classes of this season is to be found in an article in the New York Herald of January 19, 1896, written by a reporter who had attended a class on karma yoga.The paragraph was subheaded "The Doctrine of the Swami". (ND 3.340)
35 Dec 1895 S In Swamiji's class talks on each of the four yogas he adapted the most important paths of spiritual life. (ND 3.341)
36 Dec 1895 S The Vedanta Society, which he had founded in November of 1894, was still intact, and those who had drawn close to him were still eager to serve him. (ND 3.342)
37 Dec 1895 S People come in great numbers to the classes and always delight in hearing the Swamiji. (ND 3.343)
38 Dec 1895 S Sister Devamata writes on heterogeneous gathering at the classes apply essentially to the second season. (ND 3.344)
39 Dec 1895 S In the Herald article quoted above, the reporter wrote of the "well-dressed audience of intellectual appearance." (ND 3.345)
40 Dec 1895 S The interest of people attendinfg the class regularly was wholly focussed on the message the Swamiji had to give. (ND 3.347)
41 Dec 1895 S It was, indeed, difficult to distinguish Swamiji from his message. Like all great prophets, he was his message. (ND 3.346)
42 Dec 1895 S Swamiji had the power of attraction so great, that those who came near him, men and women alike, even children, fell under the magic spell he cast and, one might add, even animals. (ND 3.348)

79 Dec 1895 Notes In December of 1895, Swamiji established an Executive Committee within the New York Society which would attend to the business end of things. (ND 3. 366)
80 Dec 1895 Notes According to Kripananda's news-letters to the Brahmavadin,the Vedanta Society of New York did not come into existence until the end of February of 1896. In fact Swamiji had founded the Society in November of 1894. (ND 3. 368)
81 Dec 1895 Notes Different presumptions in connections with new born society. It was something called a "Temple Universal" (ND 3. 368)
82 Dec 1895 Notes The establishment of an efficient Executive Committee within the Vedanta Society was a vast relief to Swamiji. (ND 3. 373)
83 Dec 1895 Notes Swamiji worked very hard during December. There was a work for every one around him:Mr. Goodwin and Miss Waldo were fully occupied; Herald published Swamiji's lectures on KarmaYog and Bhakti Yog on December 22 and 29 prepared by Kripananda. (ND 3. 375)
84 Dec 1895 Notes From the ninth of December through the twenty-third-Swamiji had crammed a full season's activity. (ND 3. 376)
85 Dec 1895 Notes Swamiji closed his New York classes on the evening of December 23 and the next day took off. He went with the birds of paradise ( Mr. & Mrs. Francis Leggett) to Ridgely Manor this Christmas. (ND 3. 377 LSV 2.059)
