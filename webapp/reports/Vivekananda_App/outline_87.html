Europe: On the way to India (Dec 1896)

1 16 Dec 1896 CE Swamiji leaves England for India. Departed for continent with Mr. and Mrs. Seviers. Goodwin to join them at Naples. (LSV 2.152)
2 1896 CE The party travelled directly to 1. Milan - 2. Pisa - 3. Florence - surprise meeting with Mr. and Mrs. Hale. (LSV 2.152)
3 20 Dec 1896 Letter Swamiji's letter to STURGES ALBERTA From FLORENCE (CW SV 6.387)
4 20 Dec 1896 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From FLORENCE (CW SV 8.393)
5 21 Dec 1896 CE To judge from a letter the Swami wrote from Florence, the party reached Rome on the night of December 21 and put up at the Hotel Continental. (LSV 2.154)
6 1896 CE The party left Rome. The next move was to Naples. (LSV 2.157)
7 30 Dec 1896 CE The steamer, the Prinz Regent Luitpold, sailed from Naples on December 30, 1896, and was to reach Colombo on January 15, 1897. (LSV 2.158)
8 1896 I Because of a certain incident, this voyage was most memorable. One night, shortly after he had retired, the Swami had a phenomenal dream, which made a profound impression upon his mind, so much so that he frequently spoke of it in after years. (LSV 2.158)
9 1896 I In seeking a subjective cause for the Swami's dream, one must take into account that during the voyage, in conversations concerning religious subjects, Christianity was now and then in the foreground. (LSV 2.159)
10 1896 I The Swami had an unpleasant experience with two of his fellow passengers on his way to India between Aden and Colombo. (LSV 2.160)
11 1896 CE Nothing further of interest occurred on the voyage, except an event such as always sensational to travellers at sea. (LSV 2.162)
12 1896 I Later, during the course of a conversation in Calcutta with a disciple, the Swami alluded to this singular incident. (LSV 2.161)
13 1896 I A contrast to the unpleasant experience aboard ship occurred earlier at Aden. (LSV 2.161)
14 3 Jan 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to HALE MARY From LUITPOLD (CW SV 8.394)
15 3 Jan 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to SISTER CHRISTINE From LUITPOLD (CW SV 9.091)
16 15 Jan 1897 CE In the early morning of January 15, the coast of Ceylon could be seen in the distance. (LSV 2.162)
