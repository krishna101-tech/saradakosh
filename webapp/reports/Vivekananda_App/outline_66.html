At the Math (Jan-Mar 1898)

1 5 Jan 1898 CE Miss Noble, inspired by the Swami's ideas and determined to serve India, had given up her associations in England and started for India on January 5. She intended to found, jointly with Miss Muller, an institution for the education of Indian women. (LSV 2.305)
2 Jan 1898 CE Swamiji reached Calcutta around thied week of January 1898. (LSV 2.304)
3 Jan 1898 CE These were days of travelling and of training, particularly the training of Western disciples and admirers. They had come to India to see for themselves the land of their Master's birth, and to make closer contact with him and his countrymen. (LSV 2.304)
4 Jan 1898 CE Having left Jaipur on January 1, and with a stay of about eighteen days at jodhpur and Khandwa, the Swami must have reached Calcutta not earlier than the beginning of the fourth week of January. (LSV 2.304)
5 Jan 1898 CE This is a summary of the Swami's movements in the months now to be described. (LSV 2.304)
6 Jan 1898 CE Of his stay in Calcutta, the story is one of continuous engagements. (LSV 2.304)

17 28 Jan 1898 S Miss Margaret Noble (later Sister Nivedita) arrived in Calcutta via Madras by S. S. Mombasa. The Swami was at the dock to receive her. They were meeting after a break of thirteen months. (LSV 2.305)
18 Jan 1898 S In Miss Müller's absence she put up for a few days with a friend at 49 Park Street. (LSV 2.305)

27 10 Feb 1898 S It was with gladness that the Swami welcomed Miss Noble to India. From now on, by giving them a definite training, he made every effort to develop a deep and comprehensive understanding of Hindu culture in the minds of his Western followers. (LSV 2.305)
28 Feb 1898 S Day after day the members of the Order were trained by the Swami, until his ideas became their very own. (LSV 2.307)
29 13 Feb 1898 ME Math transferred from Alambazar to Nilambar Mukherjee's garden at Belur as the old building had received an awful shaking in an earthquake (LSV 2.309)
30 14 Feb 1898 S Swami Saradanand accompanied by Mrs. Bull and Josephine MacLeod arrived in Culcutta. (LSV 2. 310)
31 1898 0 Miss Macleod and Mrs. Bull came to live at river side house at Belur and later Nivedita joined them. They stayed there nearly 2 months (LSV 2.310)
32 1898 0 Swamiji spend hours daily with them, training them in Indian culture, heritage and religion. Trained Nivedita for her Indian mission. (LSV 2.311)
33 1898 S Swamiji regarded the coming to India of his Western disciples as a test and experiment. (LSV 2.312)
34 1898 S The young sannyasis and Brahmacharis prepared, during the afternoon of the Shivaratri day, thanksgiving addresses in English to every one of the senior Swamis, and these were read out to them at a gathering of the Brotherhood held at the Math. Swamiji was in the chair. He called upon his brother-monks to reply by turn to the addresses. (LSV 2.313)
35 22 Feb 1898 ME Thakur BD celebrated at Nilambar Mukherjee's house under the supervision of Swamiji. At the time of evening aratrika the song "khandana bhavabandhana", newly composed by Swamiji, was sung. Nivedita's first visit to Dakshineshwar, later she attended Belur math for the Thakur jayanti (LSV 2. 313)
36 1898 S On Shivratri Swamiji decorated as Shiva by brother disciples (LSV 2.315)
37 1898 S On the spur of the moment, the Swami removed the symbolic decorations on himself and put them on Girishbabu after smearing Girish's body with ashes. (LSV 2.316)
38 1898 S In the meantime Swami Akhandananda arrived at the Math from his orphanage in Murshidabad. (LSV 2.316)
