Boston (Sep. 1894)

1 6 Sep 1894 CE Swamiji arrived in Boston. (LSV 1.511 ND 2.180)
2 Sep 1894 CE Evidently Swamiji had made arrangements during his last week or so in Annisquam to lecture in Boston, for prior to this there is no intimation that he definitely intended to go there. (ND 2.180)
3 Sep 1894 CE In his letter of August 20 to Isabelle McKindley he writes, "From here [Annisquam] I think I will go back to New York. Or I may go to Boston [Cambridge] to Mrs. Ole Bull." Actually he did neither of these things. (ND 2.180)
4 Sep 1894 CE Yet in spite of a busy schedule, he did find time to compose his long, stirring, and now famous Reply to the Madras Address, his first impassioned public exhortation to his motherland since he had left her shores. (ND 2.182)
5 Sep 1894 Writing Swamiji's reply to the Madras Address (CW SV 4.331 ND 2.182)
6 Sep 1894 CE Swamiji accepted an invitation from Mrs. Bull to spend a few days in her Cambridge house. (LSV 1.511)

26 30 Sep 1894 CE Melrose is a small town a few miles north of Boston, where Swamiji evidently lectured at least twice. His second lecture, given probably on September 30, was his last in the vicinity of Boston in the fall of 1894. (ND 2.183)
27 2 Oct 1894 CE Swamiji left for Cambridge. (ND 2.184)
