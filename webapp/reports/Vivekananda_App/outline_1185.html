Ohio - Chicago (23 Feb - 9 Mar. 1894)

1 23 Feb 1894 CE Swamiji arrived in Ada, Ohio in the evening. (CB SV ND 1.382)
2 23 Feb 1894 News Reports News report on Swamiji's lecture on 'DIVINITY OF MAN' in Ada Record at Ada, Ohio (CW SV 2.477)
3 23 Feb 1894 News Reports The letter from ' Justitia' appeared in the Free Press on the first Sunday sermons preached against Swamiji. (ND 1. 350)
4 Feb 1894 CE The Christian Onslaught immediately after departure of Swamiji from Detroit. (ND 1.383)
5 Feb 1894 News reports Paragraphs taken from the Detroit Journal - report of Rev. Newman's Sunday sermon at the Central Christian Church: (ND 1.384)
6 Feb 1894 News reports In Ypsilanti, a city in Michigan near Detroit, Mr. Morey accused Swamiji and his supporters of telling only "half truths," (ND 1.385)
7 Feb 1894 Notes It is almost impossible to know how much help Swamiji was able to obtain for the work in India From individual donations, but very likely some of his wealthy and sympathetic friends contributed at least something to his cause. We know, for instance, that Mr. Charles Freer was such a contributor. (ND 1.369)
8 24 Feb 1894 Letter The following letter written by Mrs. Bagley's eldest married daughter to Swamiji after he had left Detroit the first time gives evidence of this and also that he was considering a return to India. It was dated February 24 and addressed to the care of Mrs. Hale at 541 Dearborn Avenue, Chicago: (ND 1.369)
9 Feb 1894 S Swamiji in his letter to Mrs. Hale writes about the draft and also some presents Mrs. Sherman presented to him before his final departure from detroit. (ND 1. 370)
10 25 Feb 1894 News reports Two incidents, which could have occurred at Freer's dinner party, are preserved by the following item in the Evening News. (ND 1.364)
11 28 Feb 1894 CE The Student Volunteer Missionary Movement held its second international convention in Detroit shortly after Swamiji had left. The convention, a gathering of no small proportions and no little significance, was in full sway from February 28 to March 4. (ND 1.393)
12 Feb 1894 CE The need of the Missionary convention at Detroit. (ND 1.396)
13 Feb 1894 News reports In the meantime, his first visit to the city was already bearing fruit. In the very teeth of the missionary convention the Evening News of March 1 published a long illustrated article. (ND 1.396)
14 Feb 1894 Letter Swamiji's letter to AKHANDANANDA (CW SV 6.287)
15 Feb 1894 Letter Swamiji's letter to brother disciple at Alambazar Monestery (CW SV 6.289)
16 1 Mar 1894 CE swamiji at chicago ? (CB SV)
17 1 Mar 1894 CE Swamiji spoke to group at University of Chicago, University Chapel, Cobb Hall, Chicago, IL, around midday -Modern school (VC) (ND 1.171)
18 3 Mar 1894 Writing Swamiji's letter to Mudliar Singarvelu(Kidi) From Chicago (What we believe in) on March 3, 1894. (CW SV 4.356)
19 4 Mar 1894 News reports An editorial which ran in two papers, the Tribune and the Sunday News Tribune, evinced some surprise that the ministry had reacted to Swamiji with so much hysteria: (ND 1.386)
20 5 Mar 1894 Notes The Baptists of Detroit held a mass meeting on March 5. Among the speakers was a Dr. W. B. Boggs of Cincinnati, Ohio, who had spent several years in India as the principal of the Telegu Theological Seminary at Ramepatan and could therefore, it was presumed, speak with authority. (ND 1.388)

23 9 Mar 1894 News reports News reports in Indian Mirror on Mr. Merwin-Marie Snell's letter to Indian periodical on Swamiji's lecture tour. (ND 1.273)
24 9 Mar 1894 News reports Swamiji returned to Detroit. (LSv 1.468)
