Early education

1 1871 S At the age of eight years Narendranath joined Vidyasagar's Metropolitan institution in ninth class. He was unusual in his restlessness. (LSV 1.027)
2 I Naren use tobe a care-taker of his party-mates while on excursion. (LSV 1.027)
3 I Around this time he also saved a child and its mother from being run over by a carriage and seriously injured. Hastily snatching the child with one hand, he pulled the mother out of danger with the other. (LSV 1.028)
4 CE It was this spirit of self sacrifice in Naren that made him the idol of his fellows, and so deep was the impression he made upon most of them that later on in their college days they followed his lead in matters of grave importance. (LSV 1.028)
5 CE Restlessness and kindness were two traits in Naren which can be seen throughout his life (LSV 1.028)
6 I Naren catagorically refused to learn English language toschool authorities as well as parents. Later on persuance of one of his relative he started studying English and became master of the language. (LSV 1.028)
7 CE Naren had many stories of his school days. (LSV 1.029)
8 S Naren had special liking for a monk. He wanted to be a Sannayasi. (LSV 1.029)
9 I Naren was impatience with superstition and fear and was ready to accept the challenge. (LSV 1.030)
10 I Naren was expert both in play as well as study. His friend's father blessed Naren after he replied questions on varios subjects of study (LSV 1.031)
11 S Naren had prodigious memory. He was foremost student in his class at school. (LSV 1.031)
12 S Naren suffering from chronic dyspepsia in school years, though thin, he was generally in good health. (LSV 1.031)
13 S Apart from studies, Naren as good at drawing and singing. (LSV 1.032)
14 S Naren was very good at making friends also. (LSV 1.032)
15 S Naren later tells about his restlessness to his disciple in London. (LSV 1.032)

36 1877 S At Raipur Naren learnt music from his father. Later at Calcutta naren's training in classical, vocal and instrumental music was arranged under reputed masters. (LSV 1.042)
