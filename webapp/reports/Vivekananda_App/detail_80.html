Egypt (Nov 1900)

-- 1900110000 Poem ON THE SEA`S BOSOM (Written around mediterranean sea during the return voyage to India after his second visit to the West.) (CW SV 6.180)
In blue sky floats a multitude of clouds--_x000d_
White, black, of many shades and thicknesses;_x000d_
An orange sun, about to say farewell,_x000d_
Touches the massed cloud-shapes with streaks of red._x000d_
_x000d_
The wind blows as it lists, a hurricane_x000d_
Now carving shapes, now breaking them apart:_x000d_
Fancies, colours, forms, inert creations--_x000d_
A myriad scenes, though real, yet fantastic._x000d_
_x000d_
There light clouds spread, heaping up spun cotton;_x000d_
See next a huge snake, then a strong lion;_x000d_
Again, behold a couple locked in love._x000d_
All vanish, at last, in the vapoury sky._x000d_
_x000d_
Below, the sea sings a varied music,_x000d_
But not grand, O India, nor ennobling:_x000d_
Thy waters, widely praised, murmur serene_x000d_
In soothing cadence, without a harsh roar.
-- 1900111540 0 Reaches Cairo. Swamiji suddenly announce his decision to leave for India (LSV 2.)
This marvelous journey proved to be almost the last occasion on which I was to see the swami. Shortly afterward he announced that he was to return to his own country. He felt that his end was approaching and he wished to go back to the community of which he was director and where he had spent his youth. . . ,

-- 1900112600 Letter Swamiji's letter to MACLEOD JOSEFINE From PORT TEWFICK (CW SV 8.539)
Port Tewfick_x000d_
26th November, 1900_x000d_
Dear Joe {MacCleod},_x000d_
The steamer was late; so I am waiting. Thank goodness, it entered the Canal this morning at Port Said. That means it will arrive some time in the evening if everything goes right._x000d_
Of course it is like solitary imprisonment these two days, and I am holding my soul in patience._x000d_
But they say the change is thrice dear. Mr. Gaze's agent gave me all wrong directions. In the first place, there was nobody here to tell me a thing, not to speak of receiving me. Secondly, I was not told that I had to change my Gaze's ticket for a steamer one at the agent's office, and that was at Suez, not here. It was good one way, therefore, that the steamer was late; so I went to see the agent of the steamer and he told me to exchange Gaze's pass for a regular ticket._x000d_
I hope to board the steamer some time tonight. I am well and happy and am enjoying the fun immensely._x000d_
How is Mademoiselle? Where is Bois? Give my everlasting gratitude and good wishes to Mme. Calve. She is a good lady._x000d_
Hoping you will enjoy your trip._x000d_
Ever affectionately yours,_x000d_
Vivekananda
