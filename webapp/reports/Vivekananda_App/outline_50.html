Canada and Chicago (July 1893)

1 25 Jul 1893 CE Ship stopped at Victoria BC at 11:00 am for quarrantine purposes. Swamiji landed at Vancouver same day at 7:00 pm. (ND 1.016 LSV 1.399)
2 26 Jul 1893 CE In early hours of Wednesday, ship's captain gave Swamiji a coat for warmth. At 10:45 am, alongwith other eastbound passengers he boarded a train to cross Canada on Candian Pacific Railway. (ND 1.016)
3 27 Jul 1893 CE Swamiji then travelled second class through the wonderfully scenic country of southwest Canada, where, as he later wrote, snow lay on even the lower mountains. (ND 1.016 LSV 1.399)
4 28 Jul 1893 CE Swamiji arrived at Winnipeg on Friday at 10:30 pm., He transferred to another train that carried him down to the United States and through the lake splashed hills and farms of Minnesota to St. Paul. (ND 1.016)
5 29 Jul 1893 CE Swamiji left Winnipeg on Saturday. From St. Paul Swamiji transferred to a third train which would carry him some four hundred miles farther east to Chicago. (ND 1.016)
6 30 Jul 1893 CE Swamiji reached Chicago after five day train journey on Sunday at 11:00 pm. (ND 1.016 LSV 1.399)
7 Jul 1893 CE Swamiji arrived in America with a minimum luggage, with little money and no pre-arrangements made. (ND 1.017)
8 Aug 1893 S Swamiji visited the Fair almost every day, for it was a huge and spectacular exhibition. (ND 1.019 LSV 1.400)
9 Aug 1893 S Swamiji himself was a wonder to his fellow spectators at fair. (ND 1.019)
10 Aug 1893 S Swamiji also could not escape the notice of News reporters (LSV 1.401)
11 Aug 1893 I One day, while Swamiji was on the fair ground , a funny incident occurred. (LSV 1.400)
12 Aug 1893 S Gradually the Swamiji became accustomed to his strange surroundings. (LSV 1.401)
13 Aug 1893 S Swamiji also visited 'Thomas Cook' to establish mail reception and 'Museum of Science and Industry'. It is also said that he stayed with Phelps family for short time. (ND 1.019)
14 Aug 1893 S Non availibility of credentials imperative for delegates of Parliament of Religions may result his trip in vain. (ND 1.018 LSV 1.401)
15 Aug 1893 S Even to attend the Parliament as a spectator, the Swami had over a month to wait, and his purse was being rapidly emptied. The hotel charges were very high; indeed, he found that in America everything was expensive. To make matters worse, he had no idea of the value of money and was cheated right and left wherever he went. (LSV 1.401)
16 Aug 1893 S Swamiji was not altogether friendless during his stay in Chicago (ND 1.018 ND 1.062 LSV 1.402)
17 Aug 1893 Analysis Swamiji's first six weeks in Chicago are known from his first two letters written from Amreica to India. These form the basis of further analysis. (ND 1.016)
18 12 Aug 1893 S Swamiji left Chicago for Boston. (ND 1.019 LSV 1.402)
