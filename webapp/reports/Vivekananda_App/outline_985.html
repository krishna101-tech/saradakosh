Swamiji at Baidyanath (Deoghar) (Dec 1898 - Jan 1899)

1 19 Dec 1898 CE Swamiji attended by Harendranath, a Brahmachari disciple, arrived in Baidyanath, where he was the guest of Babu Priyanath Mukherjee. (LSV 2.408)
2 Dec 1898 I At Deoghar Swamiji and Swami Niranjananada nursed the sick man back to recovery. The host realized that the heart of Vivekananda was as great as his intellect. (LSV 2.408)
3 19 Dec 1898 S Swamiji had gone to Baidyanath, Deoghar, thinking that the climate there might do him some good; but the result was otherwise. (LSV 2.410)
4 Dec 1898 0 On the whole, his health was bad; and here, for a time, there were complications. An acute form of asthma set in, causing him severe discomfort. In one attack he was almost suffocated; and those who stood about him feared that the time had come for him to leave his body. (LSV 2.408)
5 20 Dec 1898 CE At Deoghar the Swami received letters almost daily from the Math, giving him news of its activities and members. He must have been happy to learn of the Holy Mother's visit there and to the residence of Mrs. Ole Bull at Bally, on December 20. (LSV 2.410)
6 23 Dec 1898 Letter Swamiji's letter to BOSE MRINALINI From DEOGHAR (OUR PRESENT SOCIAL PROBLEM) (CW SV 4.488)
7 25 Dec 1898 News report On Christmas Day some of the monks were invited by Mrs. Ole Bull and Miss MacLeod to their house. (LSV 2.410)
8 25 Dec 1898 News report Indian Social Reformer news reporting defection of Miss Muller. (LSV 2.414)
9 29 Dec 1898 Letter Swamiji's letter to BULL MRS.OLE From BAIDYANATH (CW SV 6.417 LSV 2.410)
10 29 Dec 1898 S But alas! His hope was soon shattered, for by the second week of January 1899 his illness had taken a serious turn, and he had to wire Swamis, Saradananda and Sadananda to come to Deoghar. (LSV 2.410)
11 30 Dec 1898 S The American ladies were entertained by the monks at the Math before their leaving for America. (LSV 2.410)
12 2 Jan 1899 0 Belur Math occupied by monks. (LSV 2. 410)
13 3 Jan 1899 CE In spite of his illness, the Swami was active. The letter that he wrote from Deoghar on January 3, 1899 to Mrinalini Bose, one of his Bengali lady-disciples, reveals his many sidedness. We find him here as a sociologist giving his views on the origin of customs, and on widow-marriage, liberty, and other matters. (LSV 2. 2410)
14 3 Jan 1899 Letter Swamiji's letter to BOSE MRINALINI From BAIDYANATH (CW SV 5.144 LSV 2.411)
15 14 Jan 1899 0 Udbodhan published. Sarada Mj. Volunteered to be it's editor and manager. Nothing but positive ideas were to be published. (LSV 2.407)
16 14 Jan 1899 Writing THE PROBLEM OF MODERN INDIA & ITS SOLUTION 14.01.1899 (CW SV 4.399)
17 17 Jan 1899 CE Accordingly Swamis Saradananda and Sadananda left Calcutta on January 17 to attend the Swami. (LSV 2.410)
18 18 Jan 1899 S Miss Muller departed for England (LSV 2.414)
19 19 Jan 1899 Letter Soon after reaching there Swami Saradananda wrote to Josephine MacLeod : (LSV 2.410 LSV 2.414)
20 19 Jan 1899 0 Thakur's BD celebrated first time at Belur Math (CB SV)
21 19 Jan 1899 0 On Thakur Puja day Mayavati Ashram land purchased and Advaita Ashram formally established (LSV 2.)
