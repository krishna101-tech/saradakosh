Europe: Continental tour (July-Sept 1896)

1 Jul 1896 CE In the 1890s a trip to Switzerland was not the simple matter it is today.The Swiss Alps were probably as close to the Himalayas. (LSV 2.114 ND 4.247)
2 Jul 1896 CE Swami Vidyatmananda has traced in vivid detail Swamiji’s Swiss tour-uphill and down In his valuable article "Vivekananda in Switzerland, 1896". (ND 4.247)
3 19 Jul 1896 CE On the morning of the departure the Swami was all excitement. He had unburdened himself of the very thought of responsibilities. Several of his London friends formed a farewell party; but there was no sadness in this farewell, as the Swami was to return within two months or so. (LSV 2.115)
4 Jul 1896 CE On their way to Geneva. (LSV 2.115 ND 4.247)
5 Jul 1896 CE At the far end of the lake, some forty miles away, stands the Castle of Chillon, made famous by Lord Byron's poem "The Prisoner of Chillon". (LSV 2.116 ND 4.248)
6 Jul 1896 CE Now as the party approached Chamonix the grand spectacle of Mont Blanc opened up to view.It was here that the Swami suffered from vertigo for the first time in his life; his foot slipped more than once. (LSV 2.116 ND 4.250)
7 Jul 1896 CE Like a boy out of school, Swamiji was by no means lacking in physical energy. (ND 4.250)
8 Jul 1896 News reports There was only one incident of a slightly disturbing character at Saas-Fee. (LSV 2.120)
9 Jul 1896 I One more incident on way home. (LSV 2.120)
10 Jul 1896 ME Swamiji had a thought and a vision of establshing a monastery in heart of Himalayas. (LSV 2.117 ND 4.251)
11 Jul 1896 S While moving in Alps, Swamiji gave voice to a long thought out plan of establishing Advaita Ashrama in Himalayas. (ND 4.253)
12 Jul 1896 S Two or three years later, shortly after the Seviers had found and purchased an old Himalayan tea plantation, where the Advaita Ashrama was to be established, Swamiji would clearly define its general purpose and scope. (ND 4.254)
13 Jul 1896 S In the January 1900 issue of Prabuddha Bharata, by then being printed at Mayavati itself, the above-mentioned prospectus appeared. (ND 4.255)
14 Jul 1896 S Swamiji wanted the Advaita Ashrama at Mayavati to be a center of intensive spiritual and intellectual study which would train men and women to become true Advaitins. (ND 4.256)
15 Jul 1896 S Swamiji spoke of the establishment of Advaita Ashrama at Mayavati as the very heart of his world mission. (ND 4.256)
16 Jul 1896 S In June of 1900 there appeared in Prabuddha Bharata a more or less detailed plan of how Swamiji's experiment would be carried out. (ND 4.258)
17 Jul 1896 S Four months after Swamiji's death in July of 1902, the following began to appear inside the front cover of almost every issue of Prabuddha Bharata from November 1902 through April 1905: (ND 4.260)
18 Jul 1896 S Perhaps no householders applied, or perhaps because of the limited accommodations at Mayavati it was found impossible to arrange for their temporary or permanent residence in the Ashrama. (ND 4.261)
19 Jul 1896 S To return to Switzerland, where the idea for the Ashrama began to take form, so certain was Swamiji of what he wanted that he at once set the wheels in motion. (ND 4.262)
20 Jul 1896 CE From Chamonix, the travellers made a four-day mountainous trek to the village of Little St. Bernard. Later they visited Zermatt and also Saas-Fee. (LSV 2.118 ND 4.252)
