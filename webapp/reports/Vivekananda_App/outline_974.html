Kashmir: Srinagar (Sept - Oct 1897)

1 6 Sep 1897 Image Departed Murree for Baramulla (LSV 2.283)
2 8 Sep 1897 S Swamiji reached Barramula. Left immediately for Srinagar by boat. (LSV 2. 283)
3 10 Sep 1897 S Reached Srinagar. Guest of Justice Rishibar Mukhopadhyay. (LSV 2. 283)
4 13 Sep 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.419)
5 14 Sep 1897 0 Swamiji was received with marked cordiality and honour by Raja Ramsingh at Srinagar followed by interview. (LSV 2. 283)
6 14 Sep 1897 S On the morning of September 14 Raja Rama Singh received the Swami with marked cordiality and honour. (LSV 2. 283)
7 14 Sep 1897 S Until his return to Murree, early in the first week of October, the Swami was busy filling many engagements, private and public. (LSV 2. 283)
8 15 Sep 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to SHUDDHANANDA From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.422)
9 20 Sep 1897 S Swamiji went by house baot to Pampur. (LSV 2. 284)
10 22 Sep 1897 S Swamiji to Anantnag (Islamabad), Srinagar, visted historical temple of Bijbehra. (LSV 2. 284)
11 23 Sep 1897 0 Swamiji went on foot to Martand. He stayed at the rest-house for pilgrims, and discoursed to a large gathering of priests. (LSV 2. 284)
12 23 Sep 1897 S Swamiji set out for Achabal. On the way, at Avantipur, he was shown a temple, which legend relates to Pandava times. Its sculptures drew his admiration. In his view the temple was more than two thousand years old. (LSV 2.284)
13 30 Sep 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.427)
14 30 Sep 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to RAMAKRISHNANANDA From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.426)
15 30 Sep 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.424)
16 30 Sep 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to MACLEOD JOSEFINE From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.424)
17 Sep 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to MITRA HARIPADA From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.423)
18 1 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to SISTER NIVEDITA From SRINAGAR (CW SV 8.428)
19 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to MITRA INDUMATI (CW SV 6.408)
20 1897 S The Swami slowly made his way back from Srinagar, travelling by boat from the Wular Lake to Baramula, The delightful climate of Kashmir and his free outdoor life there had restored him, and he felt some of his former vigour. (LSV 2.284)
