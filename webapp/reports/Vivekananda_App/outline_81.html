North India (Dec 1900-Jan 1901)

1 27 Dec 1900 S Swamiji leaves for Mayavati with shivananda and sadananda to meet mrs. Savior after mr s's death. Return to plains after staying there for a couple of weeks (LSV 2.562 KM)
2 29 Dec 1900 S Swamiji's train arrived at Kathgodam at 5 a.m. (LSV 2.562 KM)
3 3 Jan 1901 0 Swamiji reaches Mayavati Ashrama. Unfortunately, during most of his stay, Mayavati was snow bound, so that he was compelled to remain indoors. He was obviously in diclining health. (LSV 2.567)
4 4 Jan 1901 S In the morning Swamiji went for a walk to the Lohaghat boundary (LSV 2.567)
5 5 Jan 1901 S Swamiji told Swami Swarupananda of his ideas about the work that he wished to be carried out at the Ashrama, and charged him to push on with it with zeal and energy. (LSV 2.568)
6 6 Jan 1901 S Swamiji received a number of visitors from Champawat (LSV 2.569)
7 6 Jan 1901 Letter Swamiji's letter to BULL MRS.OLE From MAYAVATI (CW SV 5.151)
8 7 Jan 1901 S Swamiji with Swamis Sachchidananda, Shivananda, Mrs. Sevier, and others, climbed to Dharamgarh (also known as Dharamghar) early in the day. (LSV 2.570)
9 8 Jan 1901 S Swamiji did not go out. It snowed from 3 p.m. and stopped in the evening. (LSV 2.570)
10 9 Jan 1901 S Mr. Beadon, a son of a former Lieutenant-Governor of Bengal and a tea-planter, came in the afternoon from Chirapani, along with one Pandit Kashiram, on invitation from "Mother" (Mrs. Sevier), to see Swamiji. (LSV 2.572)
11 10 Jan 1901 S Lala Badri Sah with his youngest brother, Lala Mohanlal Sah, came to meet Swamiji. (LSV 2.573)
12 10 Jan 1901 Writing In the afternoon and evening Swamiji wrote an article, "Aryans and Tamilians", for Prabuddha Bharata. (CW SV 4.296 LSV 2.572)
13 11 Jan 1901 S Swamiji was not well. The Tahsildar, with others, came to see him (LSV 2.573)
14 12 Jan 1901 S Swamiji was feeling better, and went out for a walk. Lala Badri Sah had brought a sun-dial. This he gave to the Ashrama. Through the efforts of Swami Virajananda, there was an ice-cream party. (LSV 2.573)
15 13 Jan 1901 S Swamiji's birthday, making him thirty-eight years of age. It was a rainy day, but he was feeling better. (LSV 2.573)
16 14 Jan 1901 Writing Swamiji began the article "The Social Conference Address" for Prabuddha Bharata. (CW SV 4.303 LSV 2.573)
17 14 Jan 1901 Writing The Swami wrote about the Theosophists under the heading "STRAY REMARKS ON THEOSOPHY" for Prabuddha Bharata (CW SV 4.317 LSV 2.573)
18 15 Jan 1901 Letter Swamiji's letter to STURDY E T From MAYAVATI (CW SV 5.153 LSV 2.573)
19 16 Jan 1901 S It was cloudy in the morning, and very chilly. In the evening Swamiji went for a walk with Mother Sevier. (LSV 2.573)
20 18 Jan 1901 S Swamiji left Mayavati by noon, and met Swarupananda on the way. The latter accompanied him and his party as far as Champawat, where they stopped for the night in the dak bungalow. The Tahsildar called in the evening. (LSV 2.573)
21 18 Jan 1901 0 In the morning Raja Ajit Singh fall from an 86 feet high tower of Shikandara, Emperor Akbar's mausoleum near Agra, and died instantly. (LSV 2.579)
22 19 Jan 1901 S In the morning Swamiji and party left for Deuri, fifteen miles off, while Swami Swarupananda returned to Mayavati. (LSV 2.576)
23 19 Jan 1901 S Swamiji reached Deuri at 1 p.m. and halted in a dak bungalow to cook their meal. With him, besides Virajananda, were Swamis Shivananda and Sadananda, and Lala Govind Sah. (LSV 2.576)
24 19 Jan 1901 S At Tanakpur, fifteen miles further on, where the plains begin, the dak bungalow was found occupied. Accommodation was obtained in the bazaar above a grocer's shop. Because of the constant cooking for parties of travellers below, the place was very smoky. The shopkeeper kindly gave his own cot for Swamiji; but this, being old and shaky, creaked noisily at his every movement. It seemed about to collapse with a crash! He, however, was in the best of spirits and made fun about it. (LSV 2.577)
25 20 Jan 1901 S Next morning, riding-ponies were secured for the remainder of the journey to Pilibhit for Swamiji and party. At that time there was no railway between Tanakpur and Pilibhit. (LSV 2.577)
26 20 Jan 1901 S Three miles from Tanakpur, the Swamiji and his party were greeted by Major Hennessy. On seeing them from his bungalow he came out and spoke a few friendly words. (LSV 2.577)
27 20 Jan 1901 S Swamiji and party reached Khatima at 2 p.m. (LSV 2.577)
28 20 Jan 1901 S In the evening Swamiji told Swami Shivananda that he would have to leave him at Pilibhit, where the latter was to go forth by himself to beg money for the maintenance and improvement of the Belur Math. He said, "Each member of the Belur monastery should go about preaching and teaching in India, and ultimately bring to the general fund at least two thousand rupees." Swami Shivananda bowed in assent to the words of the Leader. (LSV 2.577)
29 21 Jan 1901 I On the fourth and last day's journey to Pilibhit, Swamiji, after riding for some time, and seeing that Swami Virajananda was nervous when riding, said, "I will teach you how to ride!" After giving instruction to Virajananda he whipped his own horse to a gallop and shouted to Virajananda to do the same. But the latter's horse, seeing the others gallop, for they had also joined the race, did not wait to be goaded. For Virajananda it was a question of holding on or falling off. At all events, a good deal of his nervousness was thus overcome, and he joined in the general hilarity. (LSV 2.577)
30 21 Jan 1901 S Swamiji and party reached Pilibhit at four in the afternoon. They had eaten nothing on the way, lest delay should cause them to miss the train. (LSV 2.578)
31 21 Jan 1901 S Deputy Collector Mr. Joshi came to the railway station with his friends to welcome Swamiji. (LSV 2.578)
32 21 Jan 1901 I An ugly incident occurred when Swamiji and Swami Sadananda entered a second-class compartment of their train. Occupying the compartment was an English colonel, who rudely objected to having "natives" travel with him. Seeing that many people had gathered round the carriage to pay their respects to the Swami, he did not venture to say anything to him, but hurried to the station-master and demanded to have the "natives" turned out. The station-master came and respectfully begged the Swami to leave that compartment and get into another. He had hardly finished speaking when the Swami roared out, "How dare you say such a thing to me! Are you not ashamed?" The station-master hastily withdrew, and the colonel, thinking that his order had been carried out, presently returned to the compartment, to find it still occupied by the Swami and his disciple. Then he dashed from one end of the platform to the other crying out, "Station-master! Station-master!" but the latter, finding himself caught, as it were, between devil and the deep blue sea, had gone elsewhere. The colonel was furious; but seeing that the train was about to leave, he decided to move into another compartment himself, along with his baggage. The Swami and his disciples had a laugh over the affair. (LSV 2.578)
33 Jan 1901 S On his way to Calcutta, Swamiji learnt of the sudden death of his beloved disciple, the Raja of Khetri. (LSV 2.579)
