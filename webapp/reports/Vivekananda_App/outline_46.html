Hyderabad (Feb 1893)

1 9 Feb 1893 CE Swamiji at Hyderabad (feb 10 acc. To CBSV). Many distinguished people of both Hindu and Muslim faith gathered at railway Station to receive him (LSV 1.374)
2 9 Feb 1893 CE An eyewitness writes on swamiji's journey from Railway Station to babu Madusudan's bunglow (LSV 1.374)
3 11 Feb 1893 Letter Swamiji's letter to ALASINGA PERUMAL From HYDERABAD (CW SV 8.291LSV 1.373)
4 11 Feb 1893 S Swamijee consented for a lecture at Mahaboob College on 13th February. Brother in law of His Highness the Nizam, Sir Khurshid Jah, requested Swamiji to come to Palace for an interview on 12th. (LSV 1.375)
5 12 Feb 1893 Interview Interview with Nawab lasted for more than two hours. Swamijee discussed on Hinduism, Christianty and Islam. Nawab very much impressed. Offered to help for swamiji's tour abroad. (LSV 1.375)
6 12 Feb 1893 CE Taking leave of the Nawab, the Swami went to see the Mecca Masjid, Charminar, Falaknama, Nizam's palaces, Bashirbagh, and other places of interest. (LSV 1.376)
7 13 Feb 1893 S in morning Swamiji met by appointment Sir Ashman Jah, K.C.S.I., the Prime Minister of Hyderabad, the Maharaja Norendra Krishna Bahadur, Peshkar of the State, and the Maharaja Shew Raj Bahadur; and all these noblemen offered to support his proposed preaching in America. (LSV 1.376)
8 13 Feb 1893 Lecture Swamiji's first public lecture at Mehboob college on his 'mission to the west' (LSV 1.377)
9 14 Feb 1893 S The well-known bankers of the Begum Bazar, headed by Seth Motilal, interviewed him. They promised to help him with his passage money. Some members of the Theosophical Society, and of the Sanskrit Dharma Mandal Sabha, also came. (LSV 1.377)
10 15 Feb 1893 S Swami received a telegram from Poona, signed by leading citizens in the name of the Hindu societies of the place, urging him to go there on a visit. He replied that he could not go then, but that he would be happy to go when he could. (LSV 1.377)
11 16 Feb 1893 S Swamiji went to see the ruins of the Hindu temples, the famous tomb of Baba Saraf ud-din, and also the palace of Sir Salar Jung. (LSV 1.377)
12 Feb 1893 S In Hyderabad the Swami met a yogi famous for his psychic powers. (LSV 1.377)
13 17 Feb 1893 S Swamiji left Hyderabad. Huge crowd to see him off at Railway Station (LSV 1.378)
