North India: Bhimtal, Bareili, Ambala (Aug 1897)

1 Aug 1897 CE After a stay of about twelve weeks in Almora, the Swami had to some extent regained his health, though a complete recovery it was not. But health or no, his mission in India, and the delivery of his message, called for unflagging activity; and we next find him moving from one province to another, teaching privately, preaching publicly, and consolidating the work. He had pressing invitations to visit various places in the Punjab and in Kashmir. (LSV 2.280)
2 2 Aug 1897 CE Left Almora. On the way to kathgodam halted a day at Bhimtal on account of fever. (LSV 2. 280)
3 9 Aug 1897 0 Reached Bareilly. Again had an attack of fever. Stayed 4 days and despite of fever met a lot of people. (LSV 2. 280)
4 10 Aug 1897 I Swamiji visited Arya Samaj Orphanage in morning at Bareilly (LSV 2. 280)
5 11 Aug 1897 I Swamiji's conversation with students gathering and stressed on need of student society in morning. (LSV 2. 280)
6 11 Aug 1897 I That day, after the midday meal, the Swami told Swami Achyutananda, a monk of the Arya Samaj, that he (Vivekananda) would live only five or six years more. This prophecy, though not taken seriously at the time, came true, for he left his body five years later, on July 4, 1902. (LSV 2. 280)
7 11 Aug 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to MACLEOD JOSEFINE From MATH (CW SV 5.139)
8 12 Aug 1897 S On the morning of August 12 he held religious discussions, although he was unwell. His fever increased after taking food at midday, but towards evening he felt better. He again discoursed on religion to visitors. (LSV 2. 280)
9 12 Aug 1897 CE That night he left by train for Ambala Cantonment, which was reached early next morning. (LSV 2.280)
10 13 Aug 1897 Notes At the station he was received by a large number of people, and taken in a horse-carriage to a bungalow that had been arranged for his stay. Here he met Mr. and Mrs. Sevier. They had been at Simla for some three months, following their stay with the Swami at Darjeeling. At Ambala he had religious talks daily at all hours with many people of different creeds — Muslim, Brahmo, Arya Samajist, and Hindu — on scriptural and other matters. (LSV 2.281)
11 16 Aug 1897 I On the morning of the 16th, at the request of a professor of the Lahore College who wanted a record of the Swami's voice, he gave a short lecture into a phonograph. (LSV 2.281)
12 16 Aug 1897 I Delivered leccture to select intellectuals on regeneration of motherland at ambala in evening. (LSV 2.281)
13 19 Aug 1897 S Swamiji Visited Hindu Muslim school at ambala in the morning. (LSV 2.281)
14 16 Aug 1897 S The Swami received many invitations from various places, but he was so weakened by the fever that he had contracted on the way down from Almora that he was unable to accept any of them. (LSV 2.281)
15 19 Aug 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to RAMAKRISHNANANDA From AMBALA (CW SV 8.417)
16 19 Aug 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BULL MRS.OLE From (CW SV 6.407 LSV 2.281)
17 20 Aug 1897 CE On August 20 the Swami and his party, with Mr. and Mrs. Sevier included, reached Amritsar. (LSV 2.282)
