Punjab II: Murree, Rawalpindi (Oct 1897)

1 8 Oct 1897 0 Returning to Murree on October 8, Swamiji was joyfully received by the Bengali and Punjabi residents, and by Mr. and Mrs. Sevier. (LSV 2.284)
2 8 Oct 1897 0 The Swami was alternately the guest of Mr. and Mrs. Sevier, and of Nibaranbabu, at whose house he received numerous visitors and had many discussions. (LSV 2.284)
3 10 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From MURREE (CW SV 6.411)
4 10 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to TRIGUNATITANANDA From MURREE (CW SV 6.409)
5 10 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to AKHANDANANDA From MURREE (CW SV 6.409)
6 11 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to JAGMOHANLAL MUNSHI From MURREE (CW SV 5.139)
7 11 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From MURREE (CW SV 8.430)
8 12 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to BRAHMANANDA From MURREE (CW SV 8.433)
9 14 Oct 1897 S On the evening an address of welcome was presented to Swamiji on behalf of the Bengali and Punjabi residents of Murree. In reply the Swami gave a talk which delighted the audience immensely. (LSV 2. 284)
10 16 Oct 1897 S In the morning Swamiji left for Rawalpindi by tonga, and arrived there by five in the evening. He was welcomed by some people of note. (LSV 2.284)
11 17 Oct 1897 S Talked to visitors on religious matters at Mr. Hansraj's House in morning. (LSV 2. 285)
12 Oct 1897 Letter Swamiji's letter to GUPTA MAHENDRANATH From RAWALPINDI (CW SV 6.412)
13 17 Oct 1897 S Swamiji went to Nimaibabu's house for dinner and talked to Bengali gentlemen on religious matters at Murree (LSV 2. 285)
14 17 Oct 1897 S Delivered lecture in Rawalpindi on Hinduism at Garden of Saradr Sujan Singh, who acted as chairman of the meeting. (LSV 2. 284)

16 18 Oct 1897 S Conversation with leading residents of Rawalpindi. He gave satisfactory rsiggestion to resolve conflict between Aryasamaj and Muslim community. (LSV 2. 285)
17 Oct 1897 I Swamiji went to a Bengali gentleman. (LSV 2. 285)
18 20 Oct 1897 S On the night of October 20 the Swami was off again, this time to Jammu in response to an invitation from the Maharaja of Kashmir. (LSV 2.285)
