Belur Math established (Oct - Dec 1898)

1 1898 Writing THE BELUR MATH : AN APPEAL (CW SV 5.434)
2 18 Oct 1898 CE Swamiji along with sadananda arrived at Calcutta without prior notice. (LSV 2.389)
3 1898 S No sooner Swamiji reached the Math at Nilambar Mukherjee's garden house then he became bedridden. He often suffered from hard breathing of an asthmatic nature. He wont leave his bed from morning till night and seldom spoke with anybody. Doctor advised him to be careful. (LSV 2.389)
4 1898 S Not withstanding his failing health, resumed his old life with the monks. (LSV 2.391)
5 19 Oct 1898 0 Performed homa rite on 19th and 20th. Next three days celebrated Durga Puja. Large number of devotees attended. (LSV 2.392)
6 24 Oct 1898 0 Hari Mj. Returned from Almora to the Math (LSV 2.392)
7 25 Oct 1898 Letter Swamiji's letter to SISTER CHRISTINE From BELUR MATH (CW SV 9.107 LSV 2.391)
8 26 Oct 1898 Letter Swamiji's letter to MAHARAJA OF KHETRI From BELUR MATH (CW SV 5.143)
9 27 Oct 1898 S At the request of some of the monks, Swamiji had his chest examined by the well-known specialist, Dr. R. L., Dutt. In consultation with some other doctors Dr. Dutt gave the opinion that he must be careful of himself. A clot of blood was found to have formed in his left eye, possibly due to intense concentration. (LSV 2.391)

13 1 Nov 1898 S From November 1 on, Swamiji's place of residence alternated between the monastery and Balarambabu's house in Baghbazar, Calcutta, where he was obliged to stay for the sake of treatment. (LSV 2.392)
14 Nov 1898 S But he did not allow his illness to prevent him from meeting the many people who flocked to him for instruction. About these days one of his disciples writes: (LSV 2.392)
15 Nov 1898 S The Swami at that time was an embodiment of love. Sometimes he used to speak the words which it appears could very well have been spoken by the Swami's Master also — Shri Ramakrishna! (LSV 2.394)
16 1 Nov 1898 0 Sister Nivedita, eager to return to Calcutta and begin her work, left the company of the American ladies, and reached Calcutta on November 1, via Varanasi. From the station she went straight to the Swami, who was then at Balaram Babu's house. The Holy Mother was also living in Calcutta at that time in a house nearby in Baghbazar. The Swami received his disciple warmly and arranged for her stay at the Holy Mother's house till she could find a place for herself. (LSV 2.394)
17 5 Nov 1898 S swamiji received at the Math Shri Rishibar Mukherjee, the Chief justice, and Shri Nilambar Mukherjee, the Prime Minister of Kashmir. (LSV 2.394)
18 Nov 1898 0 After a week or so a house was found for Nivedita at 16 Bosepara Lane, where she happily lived till she left for the West in June 1899. (LSV 2.394)
19 6 Nov 1898 S Sarat Mj. Along with Mrs. Ole Bull and Miss MacLeod, reached Calcutta after a tour of some of the Historic cities of Northern India. Stayed a few days with Nivedita. (LSV 2.394)
20 Oct 1898 0 During their stay with Nivedita Mrs. Ole Bull and Nivedita arranged for the mother's photo to be taken (LSV 2.394)
21 11 Nov 1898 0 Shivanandaji returned to the Belur Math (MS)
22 12 Nov 1898 Letter Swamiji's letter to MACLEOD JOSEFINE From CALCUTTA (CW SV 8.461)
23 12 Nov 1898 S The day before the Kali Puja, the Holy Mother, accompanied by a number of women devotees, visited the site where the Ramakrishna Order was shortly going to have its headquarters. The Swami had invited the Western disciples also. All the monks were present, and elaborate arrangements had been made for worship. (LSV 2.395)
24 12 Nov 1898 S In the afternoon Mother with her party, and also Swamiji, Raja Mj. And Sarat Mj. , returned to Calcutta. Swamiji had requested them to take part in the ceremonial opening of Sister Nivedita's girls' school, in Baghbazar. (LSV 2.395)
25 13 Nov 1898 ME On the day of Kalipuja, Swamiji, mother, Swamiji, Sarat mj and Raja mj etc attended opening ceremony of Nivedita's school. Mother worshiped Thakur. (LSV 2.395)
26 Nov 1898 S From his first meeting with Sister Nivedita, the Swami had discussed with her at great length the situation of Indian women. (LSV 2.395)
27 Nov 1898 S The opening of her school marks the beginning of Sister Nivedita's work in India. The Swami showed keen interest in it. (LSV 2.396)

36 9 Dec 1898 S Afterwards a procession of the whole brotherhood was formed. It wended its way along the bank of the Ganga, from Nilambar Mukherjee's garden-house to the new site. (LSV 2.398)
37 9 Dec 1898 S This day was a memorable one in the history of the Ramakrishna Order. (LSV 2.399)
38 Dec 1898 S The disciple's record of what he heard gives a glimpse of the Swami's schemes for a national education and for philanthropic work in his own country. Some extracts will be of interest. (LSV 2.399)
39 Dec 1898 S Although the new Math was consecrated, and Shri Ramakrishna ceremonially installed there, on December 9, 1898, as related above, it was not until January 2, 1899, that the Math was finally moved from Nilambar Mukherjee's garden-house. (LSV 2. 404)
40 Dec 1898 S Swami, along with Swami Yogananda, Sharatchandra Chakravarty, and Sister Nivedita, went to see the Calcutta Zoological Gardens. (LSV 2. 404)

45 16 Dec 1898 S Swamiji announced to the monks that he would go for a short change to Baidyanath (Deoghar), and that, later on, probably in the summer, he would again visit Europe and America. (LSV 2.408)
46 16 Dec 1898 CE Swamiji left for Baidyanath. (LSV 2.408)
47 Dec 1898 CE During the Swami’s absence from Calcutta, the Holy Mother visited the new monastery on December 20th On January 2, 1899 the Math was finally moved entirely from Nilambar Mukherjee’s garden-house to its present quarters. (LSV 2.409)
48 17 Dec 1898 I Ram Chandra Dutta passed away. (LSV 2.450)
