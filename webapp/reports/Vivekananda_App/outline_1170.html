Massachusets: Boston, Metcalf (Aug 1893)

1 Aug 1893 S On way to Boston, Swamiji met Miss Katherin Sanborn in train. (LSV 1.402 ND 1.019)
2 Aug 1893 S Miss. Kate Sanborn was a lecturer. Her name was well known in America. (LSV 1.403 ND 1.023)
3 Aug 1893 S Kate Sanborn's home was known as 'Breezy Meadows'. (ND 1.023)
4 Aug 1893 CE It was through Miss Kate Sanborn that Swamiji was to meet, among others, Professor John Henry Wright of Harvard University. (ND 1.019 LSV 1.403 LSV 1.404)
5 Aug 1893 S Swamiji had much difficulty in adjusting to his new environment. (LSV 1.403 ND 1.020)
6 Aug 1893 I In Boston Swamiji was chased by a mob. Escaped in dark passage. (LSV 1.403 ND 1.020)
7 18 Aug 1893 S At 'Breezy Meadows' or on sight seeing trip to Hunnewell Miss Kate Sanborn, delighted in showing Swamiji off as "curio from India". (ND 1.022 LSV 1.404)
8 Aug 1893 S Wherever Swamiji went he made news, New England was no exception to this rule and that the papers of those towns which he visited in the pre Parliament days would contain some mention of him. The nearest town to the farm Breezy Meadows is Metcalf, some twenty five miles west of Boston,Metcalf was too small to possess a newspaper. The town next in size is Holliston, still not large enough to support a paper of its own. next large is Framingham, a full sized town, complete with news paper office.In the 1890s the Framingham Tribune, which covered the note worthy events of the surrounding country, was a weekly, coming out on Fridays. There being but few papers to look through, it was not difficult to find the following item which, small as it was and in spite of its quaintness, or perhaps because of it, had the impact of reality (ND 1.022)

10 Aug 1893 CE Slowly the way was opening up. Distinguished persons called on the Swamiji. (LSV 1.405)
11 Aug 1893 CE Miss Sanborn was instrumental in providing Swamiji with a well rounded preview of the American scene. (ND 1.023)
12 20 Aug 1893 Letter Swamiji's letter to ALASINGA PERUMAL From MASSACHUSETTS (CW SV 5.011)
13 20 Aug 1893 S As mentioned in lettter, Swamiji was fighting against impossibilities. (ND 1.020 ND1.063 LSV 1.404)
14 Aug 1893 S Swamiji was determined to make every effort to succeed in America, and if he failed there, to try in England. (LSV 1.404)
15 Aug 1893 CE After writing the letter Swamiji sent cable from Boston to Alasinga. (ND 1.020 ND 1.063)
16 Aug 1893 CE This cable, incidentally, drew forth two responses. One of these Swamiji later disclosed in his lecture. (ND 1.020)
17 Aug 1893 CE Another person who responded swamiji's cable was Mr. Manmathnath Bhattacharya from Madras. (ND 1.021)
18 Aug 1893 CE Simultaneously, as one learns from the previously mentioned handwritten copy of Swamiji's letter of November 2, 1893, Alasinga raised three hundred rupees (ninety dollars), which sum he, too, wired to America. (ND 1.021)
19 Aug 1893 CE As it turned out, neither of these amounts reached Swamiji until after September 7, by which time he had found his own way and was no longer in need. (ND 1.021)
20 20 Aug 1893 S Swamiji met Mr. Franklin Benjamin Sanborn, a Transcendentalist. He was cousin of Kate Sanborn. (ND 1.026 LSV 1.403)
21 Aug 1893 S After meeting Swamiji, Mr. Sanborn invited him to speak at an august convention of the American Social Science Association in Saratoga Springs, New York, the most fashionable resort town of the era and a favorite rendezvous for conventions. (ND 1.026)
22 Aug 1893 CE Miss Sanborn advised swamiji to avoid wearing 'quaint dress' and buy long black coloured coats as he was to speak at a big ladies club. (ND 1.021)
23 21 Aug 1893 S Swamiji gave talk to a Boston women's club. The Ramabai Circle. (ND 1.024 LSV 1.404)
24 Aug 1893 S In August, 1893, Swamiji had been invited by Mrs. Kate Tannatt Woods, whom he had evidently met at Miss Sanborn's, to stay in her home at 166 North Street, Salem. (ND 1.041)
25 22 Aug 1893 S Swamiji at Women's reformatory at Sherborn, a small town near Breezy Meadows. (ND 1.025 LSV 1.404)
26 23 Aug 1893 News Report News Report on Swamiji in Boston Evening Transcript (ND 1.025)
27 24 Aug 1893 News Report News Report on Swamiji in Salem Evening News (ND 1.046)
28 24 Aug 1893 S Swamiji was in Boston. Prof. John Wright eager to meet him, invited Swamiji to spend the weekend at Annisquam. (ND 1.027 LSV 1.405)
29 25 Aug 1893 CE Swamiji departed for Annisquam at invitation of prof. John Writes. (ND 1.027)
