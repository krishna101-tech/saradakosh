To Ridgley Manor (Apr 1895)

1 13 Apr 1895 CE Swamiji arrived in Ridgley Manor (ND 3. 076)
2 14 Apr 1895 CE Swamiji was invited by Mr. Leggett to stay at Ridgly manor. (ND 3. 076)
3 Apr 1895 CE About Ridgely Manor. (ND 3. 078)
4 Apr 1895 CE This was Swamiji's first visit to Ridgely. (ND 3. 078)
5 Apr 1895 I It was probably in connection with this much needed vacation that two stories were told in later years by Miss MacLeod, both of which illustrate Swamiji's recovery his rested, relaxed, and playful mood. They come down to us not through her reminiscences, but through the swamis at Belur Math to whom she told them. (ND 3. 079)
6 Apr 1895 I Another incident at Ridgely related by Hollister. (ND 3. 080)
7 Apr 1895 CE In the second week of April the Swami went on invitation to Mr. Leggett's country home; Ridgely Manor on the Hudson River in New York State, for about two weeks. The trip did him good; he enjoyed the countryside and the mountains, and Mr. Leggett's large estate. (LSV 2. 014)
8 14 Apr 1895 Notes In reply, Landsberg wrote on April 14 pouring out his heart, recounting his grievances, and justifying the action he was about to take. One difficulty had been the matter of eating at home. (ND 3.066)
9 14 Apr 1895 Notes Swamiji bore his disciple's outbursts with equanimity. (ND 3.067)
10 Apr 1895 Notes Reading this letter-both what is written and what lies between those meticulously penned lines--one tends to think with Mrs. Bull that the problem was indeed Landsberg himself. (ND 3.067)

12 Apr 1895 News reports To its credit, the Eagle did indeed print Dr. Janes's article, and this with a prominent heading: (ND 2.315)
13 Apr 1895 News reports Dr. Lewis G. Janes's Reply to Mrs. James McKeen - APPENDIX D (ND 2.315 also ND 2.422)
14 Apr 1895 Analysis Dr. Janes's well-documented and irrefutable article was an essential piece of work, without which the controversy with Mts.' McKeen and the Ramabai Circle would have been inconclusive and, to say the least, confusing. (ND 2.321)
15 19 Apr 1895 CE As for Landsberg, he moved to a lodging house at 228 West Thirty-ninth Street on Friday, April 19. (ND 3.075)
