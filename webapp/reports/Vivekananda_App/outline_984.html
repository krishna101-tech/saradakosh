In the Kashmir II (Aug - Oct 1898)

1 Aug 1898 S From Pahalgam the Swami and party returned to Islamabad (Anantnag) (LSV 2.374)
2 7 Aug 1898 Letter Nivedita wrote to Mrs. Eric Hammond, a friend: (LSV 2.374)
3 8 Aug 1898 S Reached Srinagar by boat. (LSV 2.374)
4 Aug 1898 S Nevertheless, Swamiji continued to instruct his disciples about India and his own ideas. (LSV 2.375)
5 Aug 1898 S In Kashmir the Swami and his party were treated with marked consideration by the Maharaja; and during his stay various high officials visited the Swami's houseboat to receive religious instruction and to converse with him on general topics. (LSV 2.375)
6 Aug 1898 S The Swami had come at the invitation of the Maharaja, to choose a piece of land for the establishment of a monastery and a Sanskrit college. There was a beautiful spot by the riverside that was used as a camping-ground by Europeans. The Swami chose this, and the Maharaja, approving of his choice, expressed his willingness to give it to him for his educational scheme. (LSV 2.376)
7 Aug 1898 S Some time after the return from Amarnath, the Western disciples, caught up in the Swami's prevailing meditative mood, were keen to practise meditation in silence and solitude. The Swami encouraged them, and suggested that they go and live in tents on the proposed Math ground, adding that it was auspicious, according to the Hindu idea, for a new homestead to be blessed by women. And thus a "women's Math", as it were, was established there; and the Swami, occasionally making a short visit, would talk to them of realizing his dream of service rendered "by the people, for the people, as a joy to the worker and to the served". (LSV 2.376)

32 30 Sep 1898 S After this vision abruptly retired to Kshirbhavani alone, there he daily performed homa, practiced sternest austerities, all thought of work gone and remain the pure Sanyasa. Had visions also Mother spoke to him (LSV 2.381)
33 6 Oct 1898 0 Returned to Srinagar, a transfigured presence. Now it is only "Mother, Mother" (LSV 2.381)
34 Oct 1898 I At Kshir-bhavani Mother said to Swamiji : 'What, even if unbelievers should enter my temples, and defile My images! What is that to you ? Do you protect Me ? Or do I protect you ?' (LSV 2.382)
35 Oct 1898 I One day at Kshir-Bhavani he had been pondering over the ruination and desecration of the temple wrought by the Muslim invaders. (LSV 2.382)
36 Oct 1898 S After returning from Kshir-bhavani Swamiji would walk in the woods beside the river for hours, absorbed within himself, so much so that he would not even notice his companions on the roof of their houseboat. (LSV 2.382)
37 1898 I Swamiji had a crisis in his life. Was cursed by a Muslim fakir that he will vomit and feel giddy before leaving, which actually happened. Swamiji angry to himself and towards Thakur. Complained of this to the Mother when he reached Calcutta (LSV 2.383)

49 Oct 1898 S Sister Nivedita, however, did not want to go to Jaipur, for she was anxious to return to Calcutta to start work. About her tour programme she had written to her friend on September 18, "In about seven or eight days I go off 'All by my lone' to Calcutta, examining on the way Lahore, Delhi, Agra, and Benares (Varanasi). Once in Calcutta, I hope to be at work — and won't I work!" (LSV 2.388)
